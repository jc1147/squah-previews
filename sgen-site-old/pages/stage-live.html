Why staging matters

Test the risky changes where your visitors can't see them.

Everyone who has run a site has done it once: edited something live, hit save, and watched it break in front of real visitors. A staging environment moves that risk off the live site. SGEN ships one with the platform.

Never test on the live site again

Try a change in staging first. Your visitors only ever see the version you chose to publish, so you can make bold changes mid-day without the knot in your stomach.

Preview design changes first

See a new layout, a restyled section, or a template change rendered, then decide before it reaches your audience. Design calls get made on evidence, not on a guess about how it will look live.

Rehearse content launches

Stage a launch, a seasonal redesign, or a template swap so go-live day is a publish, not a gamble. Stakeholders sign off on the staged version, then you ship the approved one.

Test sections and templates in context

Drop a new section or a different template into a real page and see it next to your existing content, not in an isolated preview that hides how it actually lands.

Your live site stays untouched

Working in staging never affects what visitors see. The live site keeps serving the published version until you decide to update it.

Native, nothing to install

Staging is part of the platform, not a plugin you add and maintain. There is nothing to license, configure, or keep current, and nothing extra in your vulnerability surface.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about Stage & Live

What can I preview in staging? +
Anything you would rather not test on the live site: design changes, a new section or template, restyled pages, and full content launches. You stage the change, see it rendered in context, and decide before it reaches your audience.
Does staging affect my live site while I'm working? +
No. The whole premise of staging is that your live site stays untouched. Visitors keep seeing the published version while you make and review changes on the staging copy.
Do I need a staging plugin or FTP? +
No. The staging environment is part of the SGEN platform. There is no WP Staging or Duplicator plugin to install, no host-specific add-on to license, and no FTP step. It ships with the platform and updates with it.
Is staging available on every plan? +
Yes. Staging is part of the platform, and every plan is the whole platform, all 22 native modules included. Plans differ only by how many live sites you run. Paid plans start at $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly), and the free Sandbox runs on unlimited staging sites.
