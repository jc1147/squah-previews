What Site Manager does

Provision, clone, configure, and log in as any client, from one view.

The multi-site layer is native, part of the whole platform you get on every plan, and the layer you put to work the moment you run more than one site.

Multi-site dashboard

Every site in your account in one list, with status and recent activity. See and manage all your client or multi-location sites from one screen instead of a separate login per site.

Login as client

Open a client's account to see and fix exactly what they see, then switch back to your own, with no password handoffs and no resets.

Role-based access

Invite teammates or clients with scoped permissions per site. One access model across the account, granted and revoked from a single place.

Bulk-clone

Start a new site from a proven build instead of a blank canvas. Every new build inherits your best work, so onboarding a client is minutes of setup.

Provision in place

Spin up a new site without leaving the dashboard, then configure and monitor it alongside the rest, no separate install to stand up first.

Zero plugin maintenance

No multisite-management plugin to install, license, or keep updated across every site you run. The layer ships with the platform and updates with it.

On WordPress, managing several sites usually means stacking and maintaining a multisite or remote-management service, a roles plugin per site, and a separate set of logins per client. Site Manager covers that surface natively, one dashboard, one access model, no per-site licenses to renew.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about Site Manager

Is multi-site management an extra add-on? +
No. The multi-site dashboard is part of the platform on every plan, and the agency-depth tools, login-as-client, role-based access, and bulk-clone, come with the whole platform too. There's no separate multi-site fee and nothing à-la-carte to buy. Every plan includes all 22 modules, from $39/mo, and plans differ only by how many live sites you run.
How does "log in as a client" work? +
From the dashboard you open a client's account to see and fix exactly what they see, then switch back to your own, without sharing or resetting their password. Their credentials never leave their hands, and you troubleshoot what they actually experience.
Is this the same as white-label or reselling under my own brand? +
Site Manager gives you the mechanics agencies rely on, the multi-site dashboard, login-as-client, role-based access, and bulk-clone. Branded reseller packaging is handled separately.
Does each client need their own login? +
You invite teammates and clients with role-based access scoped to the sites they should see, one access model across the account, granted and revoked from a single place. No per-site credential juggling and no over-sharing of admin rights.
Which plan includes Site Manager? +
Every plan. The multi-site dashboard and the agency-depth tools are part of the whole platform, included in every plan from $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly). The free Sandbox runs it too, on unlimited staging sites.
