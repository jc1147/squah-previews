The role model

Predefined roles, native team management.

Every person with access lives in one users table, searchable by username, email, name, or signup date, filterable by role, and sortable. Each account carries a role that determines what they can do. There is no permissions plugin to buy or keep current.

Predefined roles

SGEN ships Administrator, Editor, and Customer, with Site Owner and SuperAdmin above them. Administrators and Site Owners manage users, Editors never see the users table, so team rosters and customer data stay protected by default.

Invite and assign

Create an account with username, name, email, and role from the Create User screen. Give a designer, VA, or contractor the Editor role to author content without touching users or settings.

Access reviews

Filter the table by role in one click to run a quarterly review, narrow to Administrators to confirm exactly who holds elevated access. Audit who can do what, natively.

Login activity

Each profile shows last login and last IP, drawn from the same login tracking that powers the security audit views. See when an account last logged in, and from where.

Switch To

Site Owners can temporarily log in as another user for a support session, then return to their own account. Help a teammate or client without sharing credentials.

Safe offboarding

Edit, trash, and restore users. A removed account sits recoverable in the Trashed tab before any permanent delete, so a mistaken removal never costs you data.

For agencies and multi-site operators

RBAC and login-as-client, the spine of the agency workflow.

Role-based access lets each client see their own dashboard, not yours. The Site-Owner Switch To action lets you log in as a client for support without sharing credentials. Combine that with the multi-site dashboard and bulk-clone, and you run a real agency operation on the platform itself, no permissions plugin, no separate team-management tool.

The mechanics are live today: a multi-site dashboard, login-as-client, role-based access control, and bulk-clone. We describe them by what they do.

Questions, answered

Users & Roles, in plain terms

How many user roles does SGEN support? +
SGEN ships predefined roles, Administrator, Editor, and Customer, with Site Owner and SuperAdmin above them. Administrators and Site Owners manage users, and Editors are kept out of the users table by default, so team rosters and customer data stay protected.
Is role-based access control available on all plans? +
Yes. Users & Roles is part of SG-Core, included in every plan from $39/mo, including the free $0 Sandbox. There is no permissions plugin to buy or maintain.
Can I add a teammate without giving them full admin access? +
Yes. Assign the Editor role to a teammate who should author content but not manage users or settings, and reserve Administrator for people who need full control. Each role sees only what it is allowed to.
Can I log in as a client or teammate to help them? +
Yes. Site Owners can use the Switch To action to temporarily log in as another user for a support session, then return to their own account, useful for agencies managing client sites.
Do I need a separate plugin or tool for team management? +
No. Inviting users, assigning roles, reviewing access, and login-as-user are all native. You can onboard a teammate in under a minute and offboard one safely, a removed user sits recoverable in Trash before any permanent delete. Team changes are quick and reversible, and access management is just part of the platform.
