Why agencies switch

What managing 10 WordPress sites actually costs you

The agency WordPress stack is the same problem multiplied by your client count: every site is a separate pile of licensed plugins, a separate update cycle, and a separate login. There is no unified place to manage it, so the overhead grows linearly with every client you win.

Plugin tax, charged per client

A form plugin, a popup plugin, a backup plugin, a security plugin, a performance plugin: on WordPress that is a license stack per site. Run ten clients and you are reconciling dozens of plugin invoices and renewal dates that have nothing to do with the work your clients actually pay you for. SGEN ships those as native modules on every site, so the count of things to license does not climb with your client roster.

Update roulette, ten sites at a time

On WordPress every plugin or core update is a chance the page breaks, and as an agency you own that risk for sites you may not even be looking at on the day it breaks. Ten clients means ten update calendars and ten chances per cycle for a Tuesday-afternoon outage in front of someone else's customers. SGEN's native features replace the plugins that break on update, so there is no core-versus-plugin compatibility roulette to babysit across the roster.

Ten logins, zero unified view

Per-site WordPress admin logins mean shared passwords in a spreadsheet, no clean way to see which client site needs attention, and no controlled way to hand a junior the keys to one site but not the billing on another. SGEN gives you one dashboard across client sites, the ability to log in as any client from it, and role-based access so each team member sees exactly what they should.

"91% of WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins." Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026 report. Multiply that exposure across every client site you maintain, and the plugin stack is not just an invoice, it is the surface you are liable for.

What multi-site management delivers

Four tools built for running client work at scale

Multi-site management is four real, live capabilities: the things you actually do all day when you run a roster of client sites, made native to the platform instead of cobbled together from plugins and spreadsheets. They are part of the platform on every plan, the tools you put to work once you are running more than one site.

Multi-Site Dashboard

Provision, configure, and monitor every client site from a single dashboard instead of logging into each one separately. The multi-site dashboard ships free in the Foundation Pack on every plan. One place to see the whole roster.

What it means: your overhead stops scaling linearly with your client count. One control surface replaces ten siloed stacks, so winning the eleventh client does not mean an eleventh thing to babysit.

Log in as any client, without sharing passwords

From your dashboard, switch into any client's account to see exactly what they see, make a change, then switch back to your own admin identity in one click. No password sharing, no spreadsheet of client logins. Both the switch-in and the switch-back are written to an audit log.

What it means: no password-reset dance and a clean audit record of who accessed which account and when. Support gets faster; accountability gets stronger.

Role-based access control

Decide exactly what each team member and each client can do, down to "can edit pages but cannot publish them." Give a junior access to one site without exposing another; let a client edit content without touching settings. It is built into the platform, so there is no permissions plugin to install, license, or keep updated.

What it means: you delegate without exposure. The team scales and clients self-serve, while billing, settings, and other clients' sites stay locked.

Bulk-clone a proven build

When you have built a setup that works, clone it onto a new client site instead of rebuilding from a blank page. Start from a finished structure and customize per client: speed from template to live, without re-doing the same foundation every time you onboard a new account.

What it means: the slowest, least billable part of agency work, rebuilding the same structure over and over, collapses to a clone-and-customize, so a new account onboards in the time it takes to swap the brand.

Common questions

Agency questions, answered

Can you brand SGEN so I run it under my own agency identity? +
Multi-site management is built around four live capabilities you can use today, part of the platform on every plan: a multi-site dashboard, login-as-client, role-based access control for your team and clients, and bulk-clone to spin up new client sites from a proven build. Talk to sales about how those fit your agency model and your client relationships.
Can I migrate my clients' existing WordPress sites onto SGEN? +
Migration onto SGEN is a rebuild, not an import. There is no WordPress-content importer that lifts a live WordPress site over as-is. Two paths exist: a white-glove rebuild (we rebuild the site on SGEN) or a DIY rebuild (your team rebuilds it with the SG Builder, templates, and, for agencies, bulk-clone from a build you have already done). For an agency, bulk-clone is what makes rebuilding a roster of client sites fast: get one build right, then clone the structure forward. Contact us for white-glove pricing.
How does login-as-client work? +
From your dashboard you switch into any client's account to see exactly what they see, make the change you need, then switch back to your own admin identity. There is no time limit on the view, and you do not have to share or store the client's password. Both the switch-in and the switch-back are recorded in an audit log, so your team keeps a clean record of who accessed which account and when.
What does multi-site management cost, and which plan do I need? +
Multi-site management (the multi-site dashboard at scale, login-as-client, RBAC, and bulk-clone) is part of the platform on every plan; it is the toolset you put to work once you are running more than one site. There is no separate add-on price. You pick a plan by how many sites you run: Studio (3 sites, $97/mo annual; $117 monthly), Scale (10 sites, $297/mo annual; $333 monthly), or Enterprise (unlimited sites, $497/mo annual; $577 monthly). Everything underneath it, the Foundation Pack (hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, security patching, multi-site dashboard), is free on every plan, including the free Sandbox.
Do I have to give every team member full access to every client site? +
No. Role-based access control lets you set exactly what each person can see and edit, for example "can edit pages but cannot publish them," or access to one client site but not another. It is built into the platform, so there is no separate permissions plugin to license or maintain.
Is SGEN secure enough to run client sites I am liable for? +
A WAF and ongoing security patching ship in the Foundation Pack, free on every plan, on every client site, so there is no security plugin to install and keep current per site. On the broader posture, SOC 2 and ISO are in progress; we maintain a compliance-grade posture, we do not claim certification, and we show no certification badges. The relevant industry context: "91% of WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins." Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026 report, which is exactly the surface SGEN's native modules remove.
