A connection you don't have to maintain

A first-party integration is part of the codebase.

Every third-party connector you bolt onto WordPress is another author you are trusting to stay funded, stay updated, and stay compatible with the next core release. That trust is exactly where WordPress sites get hurt.

91% of WordPress vulnerabilities in 2025 were in plugins, not in core, and that share has held year after year (96% in 2024). Source: Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026. Every connection on this page is one fewer plugin in your stack, which is one fewer thing on that list.

One vendor, one update path. Integrations ship and patch with the platform, with no per-connector update queue.
No plugin tax. No connector license stacked on top of your plan.
No connector roulette. The connection cannot break because a third-party author stopped maintaining it.

What each connection does

One card per use case. Set it once.

Each connection lives in your SGEN admin and works for every feature it touches. Configure it once, and it is on across your site.

The deepest of the six. From a single panel, connect Analytics 4 for traffic data, Tag Manager for tag deployment, Search Console to pull search performance into your dashboard, and reCAPTCHA to keep bots out of your forms. One panel, four connections, zero plugins.

Zapier

Zapier connects SGEN to the software your team already runs: CRMs, marketing tools, spreadsheets, notification systems. Airtable

Slack

Set spam protection once, cover every form

One reCAPTCHA setup. Every form.

Spam is the single biggest problem with public forms. A contact form left open on the internet starts collecting bot submissions within days.

The Google integration solves it once. Connect a free Google reCAPTCHA in Tools, Google Integrations, paste your two keys, and save. Every form on your site is then protected automatically. New forms you add next month are protected from the moment they go live, with no per-form setup. The customer doc calls it "one of the highest-value 5-minute setups you can do for a new site."

The documentation's worked example, Acme Coffee Roasters, went from roughly 40 spam submissions a day to one or two a week after connecting reCAPTCHA. Real wholesale inquiries that had been buried in junk surfaced at the top of the inbox.

Questions

Common questions about integrations

Are these native integrations, or does SGEN connect through a plugin? +
Native. The integration panel is part of the SGEN admin itself, not a plugin you install, not a connector you license, not a marketplace listing. There is nothing to add to your plan and nothing to keep patched.
Which plan do I need for integrations? +
All six integrations are included on every plan. Every SGEN plan is the whole platform, so there is no integration-specific tier to buy. Plans differ only by how many live sites you run. Paid plans start at $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly) on Indie. The free Sandbox runs the whole platform too, on unlimited staging-only sites. The pricing page has the full plan-by-plan breakdown.
Can I connect a tool that is not one of the six? +
Yes, through Zapier. The six listed connections are native; for anything outside that list, a Zapier trigger can route SGEN events (a form submission, a new order) into the rest of your stack. For a connection your business depends on that Zapier cannot reach, contact sales.
What does "first-party" actually buy me? +
Resilience. A first-party integration cannot go unmaintained when a third-party author moves on, cannot conflict with a platform update, and is not a separate thing for someone else to patch. The Patchstack State of WordPress Security 2026 report found 91% of WordPress vulnerabilities in 2025 were in plugins. Every native connection here is one fewer plugin on that list.
Does the Google integration include reCAPTCHA spam protection for my forms? +
Yes. The Google panel includes reCAPTCHA setup. Add your site key and secret key once, and protection applies to every form on your site, including forms you add later, with no per-form configuration.
