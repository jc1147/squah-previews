Editor vs platform

An editor inside someone else's walls, or a product you operate.

When you build on Wix, your site lives in the Wix editor, a closed environment tuned for Wix's architecture. That is not a flaw. It is the category. Hosted all-in-one builders trade portability for simplicity, and for a brochure site, that trade is the right one.

The trade stops working at the point of growth: when you need multi-touch attribution across paid channels, when a client needs their own login, when "add an app for that" has quietly become a stack of subscriptions. SGEN delivers 22 native modules, attribution, forms, ecommerce, events, redirects, backups, custom content types, already present when you open the account. No app market to assemble basic functionality from, no per-app license stacking.

You own a real product. The whole platform, one price, not a rented template inside someone else's walls.
The baseline is included, not rented. Hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, and security patching ship free on every plan, including the free Sandbox.

The blind spot in every all-in-one builder

Page views tell you who showed up. Attribution tells you what paid for them.

If you are buying traffic, search, social, display, the question that matters is not how many people visited. It is which channel, which ad, and which keyword produced the lead. Most all-in-one builders answer the first and leave the second to a bolt-on: a CallRail subscription, a UTM spreadsheet, a tag you maintain by hand.

SGEN ships attribution as a native module. Multi-touch attribution and call tracking are built into the platform, no third-party integration, no separate subscription, no CallRail tax. For an operator managing paid channels, that should not arrive as a separate line item.

You see what paid for the visit. Which channel, ad, and keyword produced the lead, built in, not a bolt-on subscription.

Questions

SGEN vs Wix, the real questions.

Can I move my Wix site over to SGEN? +
Moving from Wix is a rebuild, not an import. Your content does not transfer automatically. You rebuild on SGEN using the visual builder and templates, or we rebuild it for you (white-glove). Both paths are at /migrate.
Is SGEN harder to use than Wix? +
The SGEN builder is visual and drag-and-drop, the same editing model you already know. The difference is not the learning curve, it is the ceiling. You are building on a platform that ships 22 native modules without an app-market dependency: attribution, redirects, backups, and custom content types are already there when you need them.
What do I actually own on SGEN? +
Your content, your domain configuration, your data. SGEN is a hosted platform, you are not handed a raw server to FTP into, but your content lives in your account, not locked inside an editor you can only operate within one vendor's walls. The Backups module provides on-demand and daily backups with retention.
How does SGEN pricing compare to Wix? +
SGEN starts at $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly), and the Foundation Pack, hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, and security patching, is free on every plan, including the free Sandbox. The meaningful comparison is not the headline number but what is included: SGEN's free baseline ships infrastructure that on other builders is a paid add-on or a higher tier.
Is there a free trial? +
SGEN includes a free Sandbox with no trial expiry. Open an account and build on unlimited sandbox sites (staging URL only) at no cost. Paid plans add a 2-week trial. Cancel anytime, prorated.
