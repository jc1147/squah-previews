Why freelancers switch

The three things that outlive the project

You ship the site, you invoice, and then the work keeps coming back. These are the three pains that follow a freelance build home, and the native capability SGEN answers each one with.

The maintenance retainer you never wanted to sell

A typical client site is a builder license, a forms add-on, a caching add-on, and a security layer, each on its own update calendar. Someone has to keep all of it running, so you either sell a retainer you do not enjoy or eat the support calls for free. The project never really ends, and your next build waits behind it.

SGEN fix: the builder, forms, performance, and security ship as native modules, so the live site needs no babysitter and you need no retainer to justify owning the upkeep.

The 7 a.m. "the site is down" email

An update ripples through the stack and takes the page down, often months after handoff, often the morning before the client's big day. You are pulled off paid work to rescue a site you already shipped, for free, because your name is on it.

SGEN fix: native modules replace the layer that is the primary failure source, so there is nothing bolted underneath to break on update and no midnight breakage to get dragged back into.

Every new client starts from a blank page

Spinning up a new site means the same foundation work, theme, configuration, overrides, before you touch what the client is actually paying for. The slowest, least billable part of the job repeats on every engagement.

SGEN fix: build on a free Sandbox, start from a finished template, and reuse a structure you already got right, so a new project opens from a real build instead of an empty canvas.

Native modules do the work a stack used to, so the site you hand off is not an obligation to license, patch, and rescue. You deliver a finished site, not a standing liability.

For freelancers · build fast, hand off clean

Four tools for a solo builder shipping client work

The visual builder, the templates, the role-based client login, and the free Sandbox you build on before anyone pays. All live, all native, part of the platform on every plan including the free Sandbox, the tools you put to work on every engagement.

A visual builder with real-product output

SG Builder is a visual, drag-and-drop editor with pixel-level precision and the output of a real product, not a sea of shortcodes. It runs 48 native components across 4 families, 6 responsive breakpoints with per-device overrides, and a Site-Settings design system for global colors, type, and buttons. Design a section, swap a color, pick any Google Font or upload your own, drop in a form, then ship to a live URL.

What it means: you learn one builder and reuse it on every client account. What you build in the canvas is what renders, so there is no staging-link-with-a-disclaimer handoff.

Templates, so a new site starts finished

Reusable page and section templates mean a new client site opens from a layout, not a blank canvas: select, customize, launch. There is a built-in Templates Library with 50+ section blocks, plus full duplicate and JSON import and export, so a structure you already got right travels from one build to the next.

What it means: the slowest part of a build, constructing the same foundation again, collapses to customizing one you already proved. Time-to-first-page drops to the speed of editing.

Hand the client an Editor login, keep the build yours

Access runs on three built-in roles: Administrator, Editor, and Customer. Give the client the Editor role and they manage their own pages and posts, while layout, settings, and your other clients' sites stay locked to you. It is native to the platform, ready to assign, with nothing to install first.

What it means: the client self-serves on content, so you stop being the bottleneck for every copy tweak, without handing over the keys to the whole build.

A free Sandbox to build on before anyone pays

Build the entire site on a free Sandbox: unlimited sandbox sites, no credit card, no time limit, on a staging URL, running the whole 23-module platform. The client reviews it, signs off, and you connect the domain and go live only when the project is approved.

What it means: you carry no infrastructure cost while you build, and the client takes a finished, approved site, on their own account, when the work is done.

For freelancers · common questions

Freelancer questions, answered

Can I build the site before the client pays anything? +
Yes. The Sandbox is free, with unlimited sandbox sites, no credit card, and no time limit, on a staging URL, running the whole 23-module platform. You build the entire site there, the client reviews it, and you connect the domain and go live only once the project is approved. You carry no infrastructure cost during the build, and when it is done you hand the finished site to the client's own account, where they go live on their own plan.
Can the client manage their own site after I hand it off? +
Yes. Access runs on three built-in roles: Administrator, Editor, and Customer. You set the client to the Editor role: they edit their pages and publish posts, while layout, settings, and your other clients' sites stay locked to you. It is native to the platform, ready to assign. The client self-serves on content, so you are not the bottleneck for every copy tweak, and you still protect the build.
Do I have to keep maintaining the site, or sell a retainer? +
That is the point of the model. The builder, forms, performance, and security ship as native modules, so the live site is not held together by a stack that needs patching. Security patching and a WAF are handled on the platform as part of the Foundation Pack, free on every plan. There is no per-site upkeep you have to own or price into a retainer, so the project can actually end when you hand it off.
How fast can I ship a new client site? +
The workflow is: create a Sandbox, start from a template, customize in SG Builder, connect the domain, go live. Templates open the build from a finished layout and the builder is visual, so you skip the steps that eat the most time on a build from scratch: the configuration, the theming, the chasing of conflicts between bolted-on parts. We do not publish a "ship in X hours" number, because a specific figure would be a claim we cannot verify. What is verifiable: the platform removes the slowest steps of a build.
What if the client wants to move the site elsewhere later? +
A SGEN site is built on the SGEN platform, so it is not a one-click export you drop onto any host. If a client moves off SGEN, that is a rebuild on the destination, the same way moving onto SGEN is a rebuild rather than an import. Be straight with clients up front: SGEN trades that portability for a maintenance-free, native platform with nothing bolted underneath. For most freelance clients, a site they want to stop worrying about, that trade is the point.
What is SGEN's security posture for a client's live business site? +
A WAF and ongoing security patching ship in the Foundation Pack, free on every plan, on every client site. Because the features that would otherwise be add-ons are native, there is less surface to install and keep current per site. On the broader posture, SOC 2 and ISO are in progress; we do not claim certification, and we show no certification badges. For specialized compliance, talk to sales.
