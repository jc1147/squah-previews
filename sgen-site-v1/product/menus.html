The problem with menus

Navigation should be something you see, drag, and assign.

A menu is the first thing every visitor uses and the last thing most stacks make easy to change. The structure hides in locations you can't see, a serious mega menu means another tool to wire in, and switching the look can strand the whole nav. SGEN's menu builder pulls all of that into one visual screen, where the hierarchy is drag-and-drop and the menu is assigned to a location, with native mega menus built in.

The builder

Add, reorder, and nest navigation items visually.

SGEN's menu builder lets you add, reorder, and nest every navigation item visually, then assign it to a location. The three core moves are the source, the structure, and the assignment, all on one screen.

Pull from Pages or Custom Links

Pull published Pages straight in from the source accordion, with a "Search pages..." input, or add a Custom Link with its own target. Your nav comes from real content, not hand-typed slugs that drift out of date.

Drag, reorder, nest

Arrange the hierarchy by dragging the rows, the panel even tells you "Drag items to reorder," and nest items into deep sub-menus. The structure is something you see and move, not a tree you describe in code.

Assign to a location

Assign each menu to Header, Footer, or Mobile from one panel. Edit the menu once and it updates everywhere that location renders, site-wide and automatically, with no per-page menu editing.

Native mega menus

Flag any item as a Mega Menu, with its own HTML content.

SGEN's menu builder lets you flag a menu item as a Mega Menu and give it custom HTML content, for example a Shop menu that showcases categories and a featured panel. The mega-menu capability is native to the platform, one of the 23 modules in the box, with nothing to install or license. The look of the panel comes from the HTML content you give that item.

The mobile drawer

Style the mobile menu, not just desktop.

The same builder drives the phone drawer. From the Theme Editor you control the mobile menu's styling: its sidebar logo, a background image, and the drawer and per-link colors with their hover states. The drawer is configurable, yours to shape, so the menu a visitor sees on a phone matches the rest of the site rather than falling back to a default the theme imposes.

Mobile-drawer styling is yours to configure; the panel here is illustrative of the controls, not a captured screen.

Questions, answered

Menus, in plain terms.

Can I create multiple menus? +
Yes. Each menu is its own selector chip in the builder, the verified "Primary" chip plus a "+ Add Menu" chip, so you build separate menus, for example a header menu and a different footer menu, and assign each to the locations you want.
Do menus update site-wide automatically? +
Yes. A menu is assigned to a location (Header, Footer, or Mobile) and editing that menu updates it everywhere that location renders, with no per-page menu editing and no theme-file changes. Because menus aren't tied to the theme, nothing breaks when you switch themes.
Can I build a mega menu? +
Yes. Flag a menu item as a Mega Menu and give it custom HTML content, useful for a Shop menu that showcases categories or featured items. The native Mega Menu is in the platform, one of the 23 modules in the box, with nothing extra to install or license.
Can I nest sub-menus, and can I style the mobile drawer? +
Yes. Drag items to reorder them and nest them into deep sub-menus directly in the builder, and you control the mobile drawer's styling, its logo, background, and per-link colors. The hierarchy is visual, you see and move it, all in the builder.
What does the menu builder NOT do? +
It composes navigation from your published Pages and Custom Links and assigns menus to Header, Footer, or Mobile; it is a navigation builder, not a page-layout editor. The look of a Mega Menu's panel comes from the custom HTML content you give that item, and per-item options like a CSS class or a new-tab target are set on the item rather than in theme files. The mobile-drawer controls are yours to configure. Assigning a menu to a location is a single choice you make in the builder, not an automatic guess.
Is the menu builder available on all plans? +
Yes. Menus are one of the 23 native modules and every plan is the whole platform, included on every plan from $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly) and on the free Sandbox too. The native Mega Menu comes with it, at no extra cost.
