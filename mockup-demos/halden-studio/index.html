The cotton

Heavyweight. Hunted, not sourced.

Every fabric we use is picked in person. The Studio Sweater's cotton-merino comes from a mill outside Porto we've worked with since the second drop. The Field Shirt's 320gsm linen we found at a textile show in Como in 2024 and tied up the next year's run on the spot.

The standard for the brand is simple: if we wouldn't wear it for five years, it doesn't get in the drop.

The studio

We make ten things a year. Each one twice.

Aisha started Halden out of a studio above a wine shop in 2023. The brand has one rule that hasn't moved: ten pieces a year, each one revisited in the second drop. Cotton gets ordered once. Sewing happens close to the studio. Photography is whatever happens in the lot behind it.

If a piece doesn't earn its second drop, it doesn't come back. That hasn't happened yet.

"The plan is the piece, not the volume. We pick ten things a year and put everything into them. The drop cadence is the discipline."

— Aisha Patel, co-owner
