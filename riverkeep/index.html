What we do on the river

Four programs, one watershed.

From the first bag of trash hauled out to a streambank that cools the water for decades, the work moves one mile at a time.

River Cleanups

Community work days hauling trash, tires, and debris out of the river corridor — turning a neglected bank back into a place people gather.

Riparian Restoration

Replanting native streambank buffers to cool the water, hold the soil, and rebuild the habitat fish and birds depend on.

Water-Quality Monitoring

A trained volunteer network samples sites monthly for bacteria, nutrients, and temperature — so the data, not guesswork, drives the work.

Youth & Community Education

River classrooms, school field programs, and watershed workshops that turn curiosity into a lifelong habit of stewardship.

A restoration story

One mile at Miller's Bend, brought back to life.

Before → After

From a dumping ground to a swimmable reach.

Miller's Bend was where the river went to be forgotten — bald banks, eroding soil, a corridor people drove past. Volunteers cleared it, replanted it, and have measured it monthly ever since. Today it is one of the reaches we point to first.

  • Native trees planted along 84 miles of streambank in the last five years
  • Part of 1,240 river miles restored across the watershed since founding
