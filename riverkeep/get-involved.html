Your first Saturday

What a volunteer field day actually looks like

1

Arrive & sign in

Roll up to the access point at 8 or 9am. Park, grab coffee, and sign the day's roster. Bring water and closed-toe shoes — that's the whole packing list.

2

Short orientation, all gear provided

No experience to volunteer? None needed — every work day starts with a short orientation, and all tools, gloves, and guidance are provided. You'll know exactly what to do in ten minutes.

3

Work the reach, see the miles

Plant native banks, pull invasives, log a water-quality reading, or haul debris — then watch the before-and-after. Crews knock off by early afternoon, one more mile of river the better for it.

Questions before you show up?

Do I need experience to volunteer?
No. Every work day starts with a short orientation, and all tools, gloves, and guidance are provided. First-timers are paired with a returning volunteer, so you're never standing around wondering what to do.
Can I bring my kids or my whole team?
Yes — families and groups are the backbone of a good field day. Kids 10+ are welcome with a guardian, and we'll happily carve out a stretch of bank for a company or class crew. Just give us a heads-up so we pack enough gloves.
What's the difference between donating and becoming a member?
A donation funds the work directly, and a monthly River Guardian gift keeps restoration running all year. Membership adds a voice — members vote on watershed priorities and get the field newsletter. Many neighbors do both.
When does the field season run?
Field days run Saturdays, 9am to 1pm, March through November. The office is open Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm, year-round for partnerships, planning and the occasional indoor work party.
