Our mission & history

Dana Whitfield

Executive Director — led watershed coalitions for 18 years and founded the Alliance's monitoring network.

The Alliance began the way most river stories do: a handful of neighbors, a stretch of water that kept getting worse, and a refusal to accept it. Before there was a staff or a budget, there were volunteers in waders pulling tires out of the shallows and writing down what they found. Dana Whitfield built that early citizen-science monitoring network into the backbone of everything we do — because you cannot defend a river you have not measured.

Today that same conviction drives a four-program model spanning the full mainstem and its tributaries, from the headwaters to the estuary, across three counties. The mission has not changed in eighteen years: keep the river swimmable, fishable, and wild, and restore it one accountable mile at a time.

Straight answers

Questions every donor asks.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes. Riverkeep Alliance is a registered 501(c)(3), and all gifts are tax-deductible to the full extent of the law. You will receive a receipt for your records with every donation.

What watershed do you cover?

The full mainstem river and its tributaries — from the headwaters to the estuary — across three counties. If water flows toward our river, it is in our work.

How are you held accountable?

Four ways: an independent annual audit, a published impact report, a Charity Navigator 4-star rating, and Land Trust accreditation. Our books are open to every member.
