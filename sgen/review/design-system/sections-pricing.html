Pricing sections

Three production-grade, drop-in pricing sections — not described, actually rendered with working CSS & JS. Each is a complete band you could lift into a real page. Copy throughout uses the SGEN all-inclusive framing: one price, everything in, no per-seat surcharges, no add-on tax. On the SGEN chrome — charcoal #1a1a1a, red #d51522, one 8 px button radius, the ink-tinted shadow family, motion gated behind prefers-reduced-motion.

Pattern IDs — G6 tier cards · Comp6 table→cards · I6 inclusion rows Verified responsive at 390 & 1440, headless. Zero external deps · system fonts · CSS-crafted visuals.
P1

Three-tier cards, middle featured

G6 · middle scale(1.045) + ribbon

When to use: the default close for any productized offer with 2–4 plans. Three is the sweet spot — anchor low, push the middle. Reach for this the moment a visitor's job is to pick a plan (SaaS, membership, course, retainer). The featured tier is color-coded to the one accent so the conversion path reads at a glance.

P3

Monthly / annual toggle

Stateful switch · prices recompute · save badge

When to use: when you want to nudge buyers to annual without two separate pricing pages. One accessible switch flips every price in place and reveals the annual saving. Sits on a dark band so the accent does the persuading. The toggle is a state control, not a scroll asset — it ships no sticky/scroll behaviour; it simply remembers its aria-checked state and recomputes the figures.
