Our Story

It started with twelve tables and one chalkboard.

Renata opened the first room one winter with a wood oven, a single seasonal menu, and a chalkboard she rewrote whenever the farms sent something new. People drove in for it. A second room opened across the river, then a third — and the seasonal supper-club nights became the thing the neighborhood plans its week around.

What hasn't changed: every plate still comes off live fire, the menu still moves with the season, and the chalkboard still gets rewritten by hand. Bigger rooms, same kitchen.

Few words about us

Why it tastes the way it does.

Live fire only

Every main comes off a wood hearth — oak and applewood, no gas line. It's slower, and it's the whole point.

Farm-led menu

We write the list around what local growers send that week. If it's not in season here, it's not on the board.

Three rooms, one kitchen

Each room has its own character and its own menu — but the same cooks, the same fire, the same standard.

Changes by hand

The chalkboard still gets rewritten the morning it's cooked. No two weeks read the same.
