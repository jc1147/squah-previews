Where the real time goes (the honest effort driver)

Not the concepts — those are fast (Day 1–2) and bounded (~4 hr/bespoke page). The hours that actually pace the back half:

  1. Stringing the site up — wiring the pages into a working site, conversions firing, navigation / state.
  2. Tracking — analytics events, source attribution (which handle / partner drives traffic).
  3. Checkout — cart, product detail, tax / shipping, confirmation emails.
  4. Multiple payment gateways — wiring more than one gateway path (plus crypto, included on Flagship) multiplies the integration + test surface.
  5. Ingesting all the product tables — every product row + image, sized to the tier's cap (Showcase ≤10 · Ecom ≤20 · Flagship ≤50; ≤10 for Showcase is [INFERRED] — to confirm with you). This is the input that most paces the back half: more products = more data entry, image processing, and QA, earlier.

Two of these depend on parties SGEN does not control, and they stay honest:

  • Payment processor boarding — a cannabis-branded merchant account commonly takes weeks, and the decision is the processor's, not SGEN's. It runs parallel to the whole build and can finish after launch (merch ships "notify me" until then).
  • Legal clearance for THC — age-gating, COA display, state-by-state legality. THC stays pre-order at every tier, Flagship included, until legal clears.

THE FAST-TRACK / DELEGATION CLAUSE

(written for the client — drop this clause into the engagement directly)

The deal that makes a 24-day launch real. To hit June 29, our pipeline moves fast — brand cards + web cards on Day 1, homepage concepts A / B / C on Day 2. That speed only holds if every input is settled on our schedule, not whenever it surfaces. So for each item in the lock-in lists above, you do one of two things:

PROCURE IT — or DELEGATE IT. Procure: you provide the item (approved image, product data, asset, copy, decision) by its drop-dead date. Delegate: you authorize SGEN, in writing, to choose or create it for you — and we proceed without waiting.

There is no third option of "leave it open." An item that is neither procured nor delegated is the only thing that can slow your launch — and that schedule risk sits with you, not with us.

What delegation IS — decisive expert judgment. When you delegate, you're not rolling the dice on five random attempts. You're handing the decision to a team that does this for a living. We know what's best, we know what works, we know what looks good — so we choose, and we ship on time. Concretely: we pick the on-brand image (or generate one), we vectorize the logo, we set the color and type system, we write the description in your brand voice, we choose the strongest homepage concept, we wire the gateway we recommend — and you get a clean, finished result, on schedule.

What delegation is NOT — endless guess-rounds. Delegation does not mean we throw five versions at you and you're still unhappy. It means we make the call decisively, once, with professional judgment — and the build moves on. The bounded revision rounds still apply exactly as scheduled (Showcase = 2 · Ecom = 2, R1 includes a test checkout · Flagship = 3, with 1 before June 29 and R2/R3 phasing into July). Those rounds are your structured chance to refine — they are not an open loop.

The clean statement of the clause: For each onboarding item, the client will either PROVIDE it by its drop-dead date OR authorize SGEN to choose / create it on the client's behalf. Where SGEN is authorized to choose, SGEN exercises decisive professional judgment — selecting what best serves the brand and the launch — and proceeds without further approval cycles beyond the bounded revision rounds already scheduled. Items that are neither provided nor delegated by their drop-dead date ship in their named fallback form (or are deferred post-launch), and the resulting schedule impact is owned by the client. The spelling lock is the sole exception: it cannot be delegated and must be provided, as it gates the entire build.

Why this is in your interest. The fast pipeline is the value. Every hour you'd spend hunting for one more photo or deliberating one more color is an hour off a 24-day clock that already holds a hard-blocker gate, a weeks-long external processor, and a legal gate.
