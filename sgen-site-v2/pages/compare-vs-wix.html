No. 03
Editor vs platform

An editor inside someone else's walls, or a product you operate.

When you build on Wix, your site lives in the Wix editor, a closed environment tuned for Wix's architecture. That is not a flaw. It is the category. Hosted all-in-one builders trade portability for simplicity, and for a brochure site that trade is the right one.

Point 01

The trade stops working at the point of growth

When you need multi-touch attribution across paid channels, when a client needs their own login, when "add an app for that" has quietly become a stack of subscriptions, the simplicity that fit a brochure site stops fitting the operation. SGEN delivers 23 native modules, attribution, forms, ecommerce, events, redirects, backups, custom content types, already present when you open the account.

You own a real product, the whole platform at one price, not a rented template inside one vendor's walls. No app market to assemble basic functionality from, no per-app license stacking.

Point 02

Your content lives in your account, not locked in an editor

Wix is proprietary SaaS, and there is no official feature to export a Wix site as clean, rehostable files; partial data exports exist (products, contacts, orders) but design and layout do not. SGEN is also a hosted platform, you are not handed a raw server, but your content is yours and the Backups module gives on-demand snapshots you can restore to.

The honest gradient is "more owned," not "fully portable vs fully locked." SGEN is the more-owned of the two, and the baseline (hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, security patching) ships free on every plan, even the free Sandbox.

One honest note: moving from Wix to SGEN is a rebuild, not an import. We cover both paths below and at /migrate.

No. 04
The builder blind spot

Page views tell you who showed up. Attribution tells you what paid for them.

If you are buying traffic, the question that matters is which channel, which ad, and which keyword produced the lead. Wix Analytics answers part of it: it ships marketing reports with single-touch models. What it does not ship natively is multi-touch attribution with phone-tap tracking, the operator-grade capability SGEN has as a native module. So the honest claim is precise: Wix gives you last-click marketing reports; SGEN gives you native multi-touch attribution and phone-tap tracking, no third-party tool.

Source: Wix Analytics marketing reports use single-touch models named First Interaction, Last Interaction, Last Non-Direct Click, and Last Wix FB Ads Click, per the Wix Help Center ("Wix Analytics: About Your Marketing Reports"). Multi-touch attribution and phone-tap tracking are not native to Wix; operators bolt on third-party tools for them. SGEN ships attribution as a native module, so the source travels with the lead, no separate subscription. This is not a claim that Wix has no analytics, it has single-touch reporting; the differentiator is native multi-touch plus phone-tap tracking.

No. 09
Questions

SGEN vs Wix, the real questions.

Can I move my Wix site over to SGEN? +
Moving from Wix is a rebuild, not an import. Your content does not transfer automatically, and no platform offers a one-click transfer in either direction. You rebuild on SGEN using the visual builder and templates, or we rebuild it for you (white-glove). Both paths are at /migrate, and the Redirects module keeps your link equity through the rebuild.
Is SGEN harder to use than Wix? +
The SGEN builder is visual and drag-and-drop, the same editing model you already know from Wix. The difference is not the learning curve, it is the ceiling. You are building on a platform that ships 23 native modules without an app-market dependency: attribution, redirects, backups, and custom content types are already there when you need them.
What do I actually own on SGEN? +
Your content, your domain configuration, your data. SGEN is a hosted platform, you are not handed a raw server to FTP into, but your content lives in your account rather than locked inside an editor you can only operate within one vendor's walls. The Backups module provides on-demand backups with restore to any snapshot.
What about attribution, does Wix not have analytics? +
Wix does have analytics. Wix Analytics ships marketing reports with single-touch models (First Interaction, Last Interaction, Last Non-Direct Click, Last Wix FB Ads Click). What it does not ship natively is multi-touch attribution with phone-tap tracking, which on Wix means a third-party tool with its own bill. SGEN ships multi-touch attribution and phone-tap tracking as a native module, so the source travels with the lead.
How does SGEN pricing compare to Wix? +
SGEN starts at $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly), and the Foundation Pack, hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, and security patching, is free on every plan including the free Sandbox. Wix also bundles managed hosting and SSL into its paid plans, so this is not a story about a hidden tax. The meaningful comparison is what is included and the ceiling, not the headline number; every SGEN plan is the whole platform, priced by how many live sites you run.
Is there a free trial or a free option? +
SGEN includes a free Sandbox with no trial expiry: open an account and build on unlimited staging sites (staging URL only) at no cost, ad-free, with no card. Paid plans add a 2-week trial. Wix also has a perpetual free plan, but its published site carries a Wix banner ad and uses a wixsite.com subdomain.
