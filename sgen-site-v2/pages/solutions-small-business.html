For small businesses · common questions

The questions every owner asks

Can I move my existing site over? +
Moving onto SGEN is a rebuild, not an import. There is no importer that lifts an existing site over as-is, which also means you do not drag a brittle stack of add-ons along with you. Two paths exist: a white-glove rebuild, where we rebuild your site on SGEN, or a DIY rebuild, where you build it yourself on the SG Builder starting from a finished template instead of a blank page. Contact us for white-glove rebuild pricing.
Do I need to code? +
No. The SG Builder is visual drag-and-drop, the same self-serve feeling a rented builder gave you, and templates let you start from a finished layout instead of a blank canvas. Custom Fonts bring your brand typeface, and Custom Codes are there if you ever want to drop in a snippet, but most small businesses never open it.
What happens if I cancel? +
Billing is prorated if you cancel, and your content stays yours. You run your site on your own domain, and the capabilities you depend on are native to the platform rather than locked behind a higher tier or an add-on you rent by the month, so the decision to stay is about the product, not about being fenced in.
Is it actually more secure than my current setup? +
A WAF and ongoing security patching ship in the Foundation Pack, free on every plan, and patches are applied centrally so the site stays current on its own. Because the features that would otherwise be third-party add-ons are native, there is no per-site marketplace to keep patched, which is the surface that most often breaks on an update. On the broader posture, SOC 2 and ISO are in progress; we do not claim certification, and we show no certification badges.
Is there a free way to try it? +
Yes. The free Sandbox lets you build for real on unlimited staging-only sites, with all 23 modules and the Foundation Pack included. When you are ready to take a site live on your own domain, Indie is $39/mo (billed annually, $47 monthly) for 1 live site, with a 2-week trial. You go live to publish on your own domain, not to unlock features.
Can I add a team member? +
Yes. There are three built-in roles. Administrators run the platform, including settings and billing. Editors author and edit content but cannot reach settings or billing. Customers stay on the storefront with no admin access. As a one-owner business you may never need more than the Administrator, but if you bring someone in to help with content, an Editor seat lets them work without touching billing.
