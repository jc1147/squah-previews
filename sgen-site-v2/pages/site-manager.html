What Site Manager does

Configure identity, email, socials, and analytics, all from the native admin.

Site Manager owns the per-site control surface in one place: who the site is, how it sends mail, where its socials point, and how its traffic is doing. No connector to license, no analytics tag to wire in, no settings plugin to keep current.

Per-site identity

Sets Site Title, Site Description, Email, Site Logo plus a logo hyperlink, Favicon, Timezone with a live local-time readout, Homepage, and 404 page in one Identity card.

Business and structured data

Stores Phone, Address, City, State, Postal code, Country, and Opening hours, and emits LocalBusiness JSON-LD plus the public footer contact block.

Age-verification gate

Configures a self-declaration gate as a popup or full-page landing, with custom Yes and No button text, a redirect URL, and a cookie expiry in days. Self-declaration, not legal ID proof.

Bring-your-own SMTP

Routes mail through your own SMTP host, choosing NONE, SSL, or TLS with port 465 by default, and sends a live test message before you launch.

Social profile repeater

Adds each social profile with a Platform Name, a Profile URL, and an icon from a 33-glyph Feather picker. Add and delete inline, then Save.

Built-in analytics

Tracks page views server-side and charts them in a Reports dashboard with a Distribution doughnut and a 30-day trend line. First-party, no Google tag required.

One form, site-wide effect

One Identity card sets the site, and the values fan out everywhere.

The General tab is one form rendered as three stacked cards, Identity, Business Information, and Age Verification, and a single Save Changes persists all of its roughly 28 fields at once. From there the values fan out: the site Email becomes the mail From-address fallback, the Site Title becomes the From-name fallback, and Title plus tagline become the SEO title fallback. Changes are live on the next pageview, with no cache warmup and no redeploy.

On other stacks, identity, SMTP, age gating, and analytics each arrive as a separate plugin to install, license, and update. Here they are one native admin under one Save.

Built-in monitoring

Watch traffic without a Google tag or a cookie banner.

Site Manager tracks page views server-side and renders them two ways: a raw Event Logs table, with EVENT, PATH, REFERRER, DATE, USER, and SESSION columns at 20 per page, filtered by Event Type and searched by path, and the Analytics Reports dashboard you saw above, with a Distribution doughnut and a 30-day Trends line. Questions

Frequently asked questions about Site Manager

Is this an extra add-on, or included? +
Every plan includes all 23 native modules: the control center, analytics, SMTP, and import and export. There is no separate fee and nothing a-la-carte. From $39/mo, plans differ only by how many live sites you run, and the free Sandbox runs it on unlimited staging.
What can I configure per site? +
One Identity card sets Site Title, Description, Email, Logo plus its hyperlink, Favicon, Timezone, Homepage, and 404 page. A Business card stores address and hours and emits LocalBusiness JSON-LD, and an Age Verification card adds a popup or full-page gate. One Save persists all of its roughly 28 fields, live on the next pageview.
Can I send email from my own provider? +
Yes. Point SMTP Host, Encryption (NONE, SSL, or TLS), Port, Username, and Password at SendGrid, Mailgun, or Postmark, set From Name and From Email, and send a live test from the Email Testing tool before launch.
What does the built-in analytics show? +
A raw Event Logs table (EVENT, PATH, REFERRER, DATE, USER, SESSION, filtered by Event Type and searched by path) and an Analytics Reports dashboard (a Distribution doughnut plus a 30-day Trends line). It is first-party, server-side page-view tracking, with no Google tag and no cookie-banner dependency for the page-view stream.
What does Site Manager not do? +
These settings do not include a cross-account log-in-as-a-client switch, a multi-site account roster, or per-site health scores. Those are not part of this module. The analytics is not a Google Analytics 4 or GTM replacement, is not real-time, and counts events rather than unique visitors. The age gate is self-declaration, not legal ID verification.
Can I move content in and out? +
Yes. Import pages from a JSON or CSV file by drag and drop with fields mapped automatically, and export selectively (All Content, Page, or Blog) to a JSON bundle, with live item counts shown before you export. No lock-in.
