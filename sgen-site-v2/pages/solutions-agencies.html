What you can do

Three moves that make running a roster feel like running one site

The work that used to mean ten logins, ten passwords, and a rebuild for every new account becomes three native actions you take straight from the dashboard. Each one is a real capability, part of the platform on every plan.

Log in as any client in one click

From the Agency Dashboard, switch into any client account to see exactly what they see, make the change, then switch back to your own admin identity. No shared passwords, no spreadsheet of logins. Every switch is recorded.

Surface: the per-row "Log in as" action in the dashboard above.

Give your team and clients the right role

Three built-in roles decide who reaches what. Administrators run the platform, Editors author content without reaching settings or billing, and Customers stay on the storefront. You delegate to the team and let clients self-serve, while settings and billing stay closed.

Surface: the three-role explainer below.

Clone a finished site as your next starting point

Get one build right, then clone its structure onto the next client site and customize per brand. The new account starts from a proven foundation instead of a blank page, so onboarding takes about as long as swapping the logo and copy.

Surface: clone-forward, run against any build you have already shipped.

All three run from one control surface across the whole roster, so winning a new account adds a row to the dashboard, not a new system to learn.

What it means: your overhead stops scaling linearly with your client count. One control surface replaces ten siloed setups, so winning the eleventh client does not mean an eleventh thing to babysit.

Log in as any client, without sharing passwords

From your dashboard, switch into any client account to see exactly what they see, make a change, then switch back to your own admin identity in one click. No password sharing, no spreadsheet of client logins. Every switch is recorded.

What it means: no password-reset dance, and a record of which account was accessed. Support gets faster and the round-trip is clean.

Three roles for your team and clients

Access runs on three built-in roles. Administrators run the platform end to end. Editors author content but cannot reach settings or billing. Customers stay on the storefront. It is part of the platform, ready to assign, with nothing to install first.

What it means: you hand the team an Editor seat and the client a storefront login, and settings, billing, and platform controls stay with the Administrators. Delegation without exposure.

Clone a proven build

When you have built a setup that works, clone it onto a new client site instead of rebuilding from a blank page. Start from a finished structure and customize per client, so the slowest part of onboarding turns into clone-and-customize.

What it means: the least billable part of agency work, rebuilding the same structure over and over, collapses to clone-and-customize, so a new account onboards in the time it takes to swap the brand.

For agencies · common questions

Agency questions, answered

Can I run SGEN behind my own client relationships? +
Multi-site management is built around four live capabilities you use today, part of the platform on every plan: a multi-site dashboard, login-as-client, three built-in roles for your team and clients, and clone-forward to spin up new client sites from a proven build. For how a white-label or reseller arrangement fits your agency model and your client relationships, talk to sales.
Can I bring my clients' existing sites onto SGEN? +
Moving a client onto SGEN is a rebuild, not an import. Two paths exist: a white-glove rebuild (we rebuild the site on SGEN) or a DIY rebuild (your team rebuilds it with the SG Builder and templates, and clones forward from a build you have already done). For a roster, clone-forward is what makes it fast: get one build right, then clone the structure across accounts. Contact us for white-glove pricing.
How does login-as-client work? +
From your dashboard you switch into a client account to see exactly what they see, make the change you need, then switch back to your own admin identity in one click. You do not share or store the client's password. Every switch is recorded.
How do roles work for my team and my clients? +
There are three built-in roles. Administrators run the platform, including sites, settings, and billing. Editors author and edit content but cannot reach settings or billing. Customers stay on the storefront with no admin access. You assign a role per person, so the team works without touching billing and clients self-serve without reaching platform controls.
Which plan do I need to run client sites? +
The whole platform, including multi-site management (the dashboard, login-as-client, the three roles, and clone-forward), is part of every plan with nothing gated. You pick a plan by how many live sites you run: Studio covers 3, Scale covers 10, and Enterprise runs an unlimited portfolio at a published price you check out on, not a sales call. A free Sandbox gives you unlimited staging sites to build in. The Foundation Pack (hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, security patching) is part of every plan, even the free Sandbox.
What is SGEN's security posture for client sites I am liable for? +
A WAF and ongoing security patching ship in the Foundation Pack, free on every plan, on every client site, with every module running natively in the platform. Each site is patched centrally, so keeping the roster current is part of the platform rather than a per-site chore. On the broader posture, SOC 2 and ISO are in progress; we do not claim certification, and we show no certification badges.
