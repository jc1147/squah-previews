The list below is not a feature tour. It is your current plugin bill, itemized, then deleted.

Every capability below is native, first-party platform code, shipped, updated, and tested by SGEN. Nothing here is an add-on you install, license, or patch yourself.

The whole point

23 modules. Zero plugin licenses. One vendor to call when something breaks.

The WordPress plugin market exists because WordPress shipped without these capabilities. Every plugin you license is a workaround for a gap the platform never closed, maintained by a developer you have never met, on a release schedule you do not control. SGEN was built without those gaps. The 23 modules above are not add-ons layered onto a CMS. They are the platform: they ship with your plan, they update when SGEN updates, and they are tested together.

When something breaks, you make one call, not a triage tour across a dozen vendors arguing over whose update caused it. And the whole maintenance surface of a plugin stack, the updates, the compatibility checks, the patches, is a surface SGEN users simply do not carry, not because plugins are blocked, but because the capabilities they provide are native.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about SGEN modules

How many modules does SGEN include? +
23 native modules across 7 categories: Build & Design (4), Content & Publishing (4), Growth & Conversion (4), SEO & Performance (4), Analytics & Attribution (3), Security & Compliance (2), and Operations (2).
Are all 23 modules included in every plan? +
Yes. All 23 native modules are included in every paid plan, with no feature gating. There are no à-la-carte modules and nothing priced separately; the whole platform ships in the box. The free Sandbox includes all 23 modules too, on unlimited staging-URL sites. Paid plans differ only by how many live sites you can publish: Indie 1, Studio 3, Scale 10, Enterprise unlimited.
Is this just WordPress with the plugins pre-installed? +
No, and the distinction is the whole point. SGEN's 23 modules are native, first-party platform code: they are not plugin files, so they carry no separate install, compatibility, or patching surface. Pre-installed plugins, by contrast, still live in wp-content/plugins/ and still carry every bit of the maintenance and security overhead any other plugin does.
Can I add third-party plugins to SGEN? +
SGEN is not a WordPress site and does not have a plugin ecosystem. In its place you get 23 native modules covering the same capability categories as the WordPress plugin market. When you need something beyond a module, the Custom Codes module injects head and body scripts, and the Integrations module connects to Zapier for workflow automation.
What if SGEN does not have a module for what I need? +
Start by mapping your current plugin list against the 23 modules above; most common plugin categories already correspond to a native module. For genuine gaps, Custom Codes handles script injection and Integrations connects to Zapier, Airtable, Slack, and Google services. For specific requirements beyond that, contact sales to discuss the roadmap.
