Multi-site management

Run dozens of sites as a portfolio, not a pile of logins.

For organizations running many sites, the platform provides the multi-site dashboard, login-as-client access, role-based access control, and bulk site cloning. Provision, configure, and monitor every site from one place; give each team or client scoped access; and stand up a new site by cloning a finished one instead of rebuilding.

Multi-site dashboard

Provision, configure, and monitor every site from a single control surface instead of logging into each one separately. The whole portfolio in one place.

What it means: operational overhead stops scaling linearly with site count. The eleventh site is not an eleventh thing to babysit.

Login-as-client

Switch into any account to see exactly what they see, make a change, then step back to your own admin in one click. No password sharing, and no separate login to juggle per site.

What it means: faster support without handing out passwords or logging into each site separately.

Role-based access control

Access is set by one of three predefined roles, not a permission matrix you wire up. Give a teammate the Editor role to author content without reaching Users or settings, and reserve Administrator for the people who run the site. Scope access to one site without exposing another, with no permissions plugin to license or maintain.

What it means: you delegate by assigning a role, and other sites and settings stay out of reach.

Bulk-clone a proven build

When a setup works, clone it onto a new site instead of rebuilding from a blank page. Start from a finished structure and customize per site.

What it means: the slowest, least billable part of the work collapses to clone-and-customize.

The case

What enterprise teams actually get.

Three things separate SGEN at the enterprise tier, and each one replaces a cost or a risk you are carrying now.

The security tax starts at zero

Hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, security patching, and the multi-site dashboard are included on every plan, including the $0 Sandbox. With WP Engine, the security and performance tax starts where ours is free: their Global Edge Security is a paid add-on (third-party est. ~$300 to $450/yr, always labeled an estimate, never WP Engine's official price).

Replaces: a paid security tier you would otherwise carry per site.

Native attribution, no CallRail tax

Multi-touch attribution and call tracking ship native. WordPress needs CallRail and a stack of plugins for this; WP Engine has nothing here. At enterprise scale that is a recurring cost removed, not just a feature gained: one fewer marketing vendor, one fewer invoice, attribution that is part of the platform instead of a fragile add-on.

Removes: a marketing layer your team is already paying for.

Immutable stability, no update roulette

Native features replace the plugins that break on update. At enterprise scale, "the plugin updated and the site broke" stops being a fire drill, because there is no plugin layer to break.

Removes: the core-versus-plugin compatibility roulette across the roster.

For an enterprise team the math is simple: fewer vendors, fewer invoices, fewer failure points, and a security tax that starts at zero.

Security posture

Enterprise-grade by default. Honest about what's certified.

Every Enterprise build ships on the same hardened platform, and we are straight about where our certifications stand.

In every build, free in the Foundation Pack

Firewalled, encrypted, patched, audit-logged

WAF. A web application firewall on every site.
SSL. Encryption by default.
CDN. Global delivery.
Audit-ready logging. Including a per-session consent log for audit.
Security patching. Handled at the platform level, so the plugins that are the top failure source are not yours to patch.
Certifications, the honest version

HIPAA compliant. SOC 2 and ISO in progress.

SGEN is HIPAA compliant for healthcare: it runs on a single secure database with data protection and does not host patients' records or PHI, so the protected data stays under the clinic's control while the public site runs on a firewalled, centrally-patched platform. SOC 2 and ISO are in progress; for those we maintain a compliance-grade posture, show no badges, and never claim a certification we have not earned.

What it means: hand your security team a build that is already firewalled, encrypted, patched, and audit-logged, and get a straight answer on exactly where certifications stand. That is the posture that survives a security review.

Every feature ships native, so there is no third-party plugin layer to patch, audit, or get breached through. The part of a WordPress stack that most often fails a security review, the plugins, is simply not part of this architecture.

What security and compliance posture does SGEN have? +
Every Enterprise build includes WAF, SSL, CDN, audit-ready logging, and platform-level security patching, free in the Foundation Pack. SGEN is HIPAA compliant for healthcare: the public site runs on a firewalled, centrally-patched platform, and protected records and PHI stay under the clinic's control rather than being hosted on SGEN. You can start on Enterprise self-serve at the published price. For dedicated onboarding support, talk to sales to scope it. We do not promise a specific onboarding timeline, team, or deliverable list until the scope is confirmed with you.
What is included in Enterprise, do I have to add anything? +
Nothing to add. Enterprise is the whole platform: all 23 modules, the Foundation Pack, and the full dashboard, included exactly as on every plan. There are no Suites, no a-la-carte add-ons, and no per-plan feature gating; the only thing Enterprise changes is that you run an unlimited number of live sites. For genuinely custom requirements, bespoke SLA terms or dedicated onboarding, talk to sales, but that is a conversation about extras, not a separate pricing tier.
Can our team manage multiple sites and control client access? +
Yes. The multi-site dashboard, login-as-client access, role-based access control, and bulk site cloning are live. You provision and monitor every site from one place, switch into any account to make a change and switch back, and set exactly what each team member or client can do.
