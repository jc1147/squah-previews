Native module · Ecommerce

Sell natively. No WooCommerce required.

SGEN runs a native catalog grid, product pages, cart, a live header mini-cart, checkout, orders, and refunds in one platform, in the same admin as your pages and posts. The real funnel by route: a catalog at /products with AJAX sort, filter, and category facets, a single product at /product/<slug> with variation selectors and add-to-cart, a /cart with line-item quantity updates, a coupon field, and totals, and a checkout that takes Stripe and PayPal.

Build a product the way you build a page. Set price and sale price, add variants with their own stock, configure shipping and tax, take payment, then manage orders and refunds from the same dashboard. One admin, one login, one update path, with nothing to install, license, or keep current as a separate plugin.

Product detail

A 30-plus-field editor, not a six-field card.

SGEN's product editor breaks a product into variants, each with its own price and stock, across a tabbed form most "CMS with a store" tools never ship. Switch a product between simple and variable, build the variation matrix from attributes and terms, set inventory with stock, stock status, and sold-individually, add physical shipping with weight and dimensions and a shipping class, flag virtual and downloadable, add upsells, cross-sells, and purchase notes, and fill a per-product SEO tab and review toggles. Stock decrements automatically as orders come in and is held at checkout, so you do not oversell the last unit.

The operating engine

A full store-operations console, configured in the box.

SGEN configures shipping, tax, checkout fields, and payments in one console of eight tabs, with helper text under every field: General, Purchase Flow, Checkout Fields, Layout & SEO, Shipping, Taxes, Payment Gateways, and Product Tabs. Shipping ships four built-in methods. Taxes runs inclusive or exclusive pricing with a configurable label and location-based rules. Checkout Fields is a per-field table to enable, disable, relabel, or require every billing and shipping field. The operational layer that usually means three more extensions is part of the store config.

Inventory that holds

Per-variant stock counts decrement automatically on each order and are reserved at checkout, so you do not oversell the last unit. Restock a variant and it returns to the storefront.

Coupons with limits

Run a percentage off or a fixed amount off the cart, with usage caps total and per customer, allowed-user targeting, an expiry date, and redemption tracking, so you see exactly how a code performed.

Checkout you control

Enable, disable, relabel, or require every billing and shipping field, choose grid or list with items per page and per row, and set what happens after add-to-cart: a drawer, popup, toast, or redirect.

Know your buyers

Each product carries the list of customers who bought it, a built-in buyer view, so you can see who to email about a restock or a new release without exporting to another tool.

Structured export

Export order data as structured CSV the moment finance asks for it, by date range and customer, with line items and totals, so reconciliation does not mean a second tool stitched onto your catalog.

Online and offline payment

Take cards through Stripe and PayPal with separate Sandbox and Live modes, or switch on a manual path where an order lands in Awaiting Payment with your bank-transfer instructions in the confirmation email.

Order operations

Move an order through nine statuses, refund per line item to the gateway.

The orders cockpit moves every order through a 9-status pipeline and issues per-line-item refunds straight to Stripe or PayPal. The list carries nine status tabs with live counts, bulk transitions that skip orders already in the target status, a search across customer, email, product, and address fields, and a 3-month date window with a separate CSV export for longer ranges. Open one order for a two-column cockpit: the invoice on the left, the status select and the inline refund form on the right.

Questions

Common questions about Ecommerce

Can I sell product variants like size and color? +
Yes. Switch a product to variable, build the variation matrix from attributes and terms, and give each variant its own price and its own stock count. Stock decrements as orders come in and is held at checkout, so you do not oversell the last unit.
How do shipping and tax work? +
Shipping supports four built-in methods: flat rate, free shipping over a threshold, local pickup with instructions, and weight-based rates per kg. For tax, you choose inclusive or exclusive pricing, set a tax label like VAT or GST, and add rates per location. You configure both in store settings, alongside checkout and payment.
Can I issue refunds and track orders? +
Yes. Move any order through its 9-status pipeline (Completed, Shipped, Pending Online Payment, Awaiting Payment, Cancelled, Failed, Refunded, plus All and Trash), then open one to issue a full or partial refund per line item to Stripe or PayPal, with optional restock and a manual-or-gateway choice. Add an internal note for your team and export structured CSV by date range and customer whenever you need it.
Does SGEN support subscriptions or recurring payments? +
SGEN takes one-time purchases through Stripe and PayPal natively. For subscription or recurring billing, contact sales to discuss your requirements. We would rather scope it with you than overstate it here.
Can I move my existing WooCommerce store to SGEN? +
Moving to SGEN is a rebuild, not an import. There is no product-import tool, and we will not pretend otherwise. You have two real paths: a white-glove rebuild, where we rebuild your store for a quoted price, or a DIY rebuild on SGEN using the builder and templates. The migration page explains both.
Is ecommerce included, or do I pay extra for it? +
Ecommerce is a native module included in every paid plan. There is nothing extra to buy and no Ecommerce add-on. The free Sandbox includes it too, on staging-URL sites. Every plan is the whole platform; you choose a plan by how many live sites you run: Indie 1, Studio 3, Scale 10, Enterprise unlimited.
