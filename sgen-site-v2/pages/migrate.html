Moving from WordPress

Moving to SGEN is a rebuild. Not an import.

SGEN does not import your WordPress site. It rebuilds it: faster, cleaner, and without the plugin stack you came here to leave behind.

An import would carry your problems across with your content: the plugin licenses, the theme hacks, the slow render, the update anxiety. A rebuild leaves all of that on the other side of the door. There are two real ways to do it, and you pick the one that fits your team.

The case for starting clean

An import would import your problems. A rebuild starts from clean architecture.

Your content is worth keeping. Your old architecture is not.

A WordPress site is rarely just WordPress. It is WordPress plus a theme, plus a page builder, plus the dozen plugins you bolted on over the years to do the things the platform could not do natively: forms, popups, SEO, redirects, backups, attribution. Carry all of that across in an import and you carry the technical debt with it.

Rebuilding on SGEN means none of that comes with you. You arrive on a platform where those capabilities are already native: 22 modules across 7 categories, no plugin licenses to stack, no plugin roulette on every update.

Keep your rankings

A rebuild does not have to cost you a single ranking.

Map your old URLs to your new ones before you launch. The link equity comes with you.

The fear with any rebuild is the same: the URLs change, the old links 404, and the rankings you spent months earning evaporate. SGEN ships a native Redirects manager so that never happens. Set up 301 (permanent) and 302 (temporary) rules that forward every old URL to its new home, and test each one with the built-in Test URL dry-run before you go live.

301 and 302 rules, native. Permanent moves keep your SEO equity, temporary detours hold the old URL in the index.

Questions

Questions about moving to SGEN

Can I import my WordPress site into SGEN? +
No, and that is deliberate. SGEN does not import WordPress sites, because an import would carry your plugin stack, theme debt, and slow render across with your content. Moving to SGEN is a rebuild: either we rebuild it for you (white-glove) or you rebuild it on the free Sandbox (DIY). You arrive on clean, native architecture instead of a copy of the problems you were trying to escape.
Will my content transfer? +
It is a rebuild, so your content is recreated on SGEN rather than imported automatically. On the DIY path you recreate it yourself using SG Builder and templates. On the white-glove path we rebuild it for you. Either way you end up with your content on SGEN, without the plugin stack that came with it on WordPress.
How long does a rebuild take? +
It depends on the size of your site. For a white-glove rebuild, the timeline is scoped with you, so talk to sales. For a DIY rebuild, you set the pace: start free on the Sandbox today and launch whenever you are ready, with no time limit on the free tier.
What happens to my Google rankings when the URLs change? +
They follow you, if you set up redirects before launch. SGEN's native Redirects module lets you map every old URL to its new one with 301 (permanent) rules, and the Test URL dry-run confirms each rule matches before you go live. Map old to new, test, launch, and your link equity transfers with your rebuild. See redirects for the full module.
What will it cost to be on SGEN after the rebuild? +
SGEN is one all-inclusive platform priced by how many live sites you run. Every plan includes the whole platform, all 22 modules, with nothing to add on. The free Sandbox runs everything on unlimited sandbox sites (staging URL only). Paid plans are Indie (1 site) at $39/mo annual ($47 monthly), Studio (3 sites) at $97/$117, Scale (10 sites) at $297/$333, and Enterprise (unlimited sites) at $497/$577. Enterprise is the published-price ceiling, not a sales call. There is a 2-week trial and you can cancel anytime, prorated. The white-glove rebuild itself is a separate, sales-quoted service, so talk to sales. See pricing for plan detail.
