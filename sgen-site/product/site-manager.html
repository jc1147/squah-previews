Native module · Site Manager

Site Manager: manage multiple websites from one native admin.

Site Manager runs every site you own from one admin: a roster of real, independent sites, each with its own database, content, and settings, not a shared-DB sub-site. From that roster you clone a site to another, restore from a different site, push live to staging and staging back to live, and back up then restore, each one click, server-side, and point-in-time. Underneath each row sits the full per-site control center, identity, SMTP, socials, analytics, and backups, on every plan with nothing to install first.

Clone and restore across sites

Clone a site, or restore from another, in one click.

Site Manager clones an entire site, content, settings, and media, onto another site in one click, so spinning up the next project starts from a finished one. It restores from a different site the same way: pick a source, click once, and the target site takes on that site's point-in-time state. Both run server-side, with no file to download and re-upload.

A safety net per site

Back up a site, then restore it, in one click each.

Inside every site

One Identity card sets the site, and the values fan out everywhere.

Open any site and the General tab is one form rendered as three stacked cards, Identity, Business Information, and Age Verification, and a single Save Changes persists all of its roughly 28 fields at once. From there the values fan out: the site Email becomes the mail From-address fallback, the Site Title becomes the From-name fallback, and Title plus tagline become the SEO title fallback. Changes are live on the next pageview, with no cache warmup and no redeploy.

Identity, SMTP, age gating, and first-party analytics are native modules in this one admin, set once under one Save and live on the next pageview. Nothing to install, license, or update.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about Site Manager

Are these one click, or a manual export and re-upload? +
One click, server-side. Clone a site to another, restore from a different site, push live to staging and staging back to live, and back up then restore are all single actions inside the admin. There is no file to export, download, and re-upload; the platform copies the site for you, point-in-time.
Is each site a real separate site, or a shared sub-site? +
Each site is a real, independent site with its own database, its own content, and its own settings. They share one admin to manage from, not one database. Cloning copies a whole site onto another; it does not fork a shared network.
How are backups handled? +
On demand and point-in-time. Snapshot a whole site, its database and media, into one backup before a risky change, and Restore returns it to that exact state. Backups are taken when you ask; there is no scheduled or off-site copy, and they are managed inside the admin, not downloaded.
What does the per-site control center configure? +
One Identity card sets Site Title, Description, Email, Logo plus its hyperlink, Favicon, Timezone, Homepage, and 404 page; a Business card stores address and hours and emits LocalBusiness JSON-LD; an Age Verification card adds a popup or full-page gate. One Save persists all of its roughly 28 fields, live on the next pageview. You can also point your own SMTP and import or export pages as JSON or CSV.
What does the built-in analytics show, and is it Google Analytics? +
It is first-party and server-side: a page-view read per site, not a Google Analytics 4 or GTM replacement, not real-time, and it counts events rather than unique visitors. The age gate is self-declaration, not legal ID verification.
How is it priced? +
By how many live sites you run, not per feature or per site add-on. Every plan is the whole platform with all 23 native modules. Paid plans start at $39/mo billed annually, $47 monthly, and the free Sandbox runs everything on unlimited staging sites before you pay anything.
