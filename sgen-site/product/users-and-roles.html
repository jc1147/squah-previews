User roles and permissions: one native users table, three roles.

SGEN manages user roles and permissions from a single Users list, with five filter pills (All Users, Administrator, Editor, Customer, Trash) carrying live counts, an eight-column table that tracks last login and last IP per account, and bulk actions across the whole roster. Assign one of three roles, search by username, email, name, or signup date, and soft-delete to a Trash you can restore from.

User management runs natively in your dashboard, on every plan, with no add-on to license and no permission matrix to maintain.

The role model

Three predefined roles, one native users table.

SGEN ships three roles, no more and no fewer: Administrator, Editor, and Customer. Every account lives in one users table, searchable by username, email, name, or signup date and filterable by role through the five pills. You assign access by picking a role, not by toggling a matrix of capabilities.

Level 1

Administrator

Full control. Administrators sign into the admin panel, manage every user, and reach every module. Reserve this role for the people who run the site.

Routes to /sg-admin/
Level 2

Editor

Content, not control. Editors get a reduced admin sidebar with no Users node, and hit an "Access has been restricted" page on privileged routes. Give a designer, VA, or contractor this role to author content without touching users or settings.

Reduced sidebar, no Users node
Level 0

Customer

Storefront only. Customers are redirected to the account portal and never see the admin panel. This is the role your store members and registered visitors hold.

Routes to /account/
Create and assign

Create an account, pick a role, done.

SGEN's Add User form creates a profile and sets the role in one place, no email-invite round trip to wait on. Fill in the details, choose Administrator, Editor, or Customer from the Role dropdown, and save. Username is alpha-numeric, five to twelve characters, and unique; email must be valid and unique, so accounts stay clean from the start.

Safe offboarding

Trash is recoverable until you delete permanently.

SGEN soft-deletes a removed account to a Trash tab instead of erasing it. The row keeps its data, carries a red Trash tag, and shows a dash for Last IP and Last Login. Choose Restore to bring it back with its role intact, or Delete Permanently when you are sure. Remove the wrong account and you can recover it from Trash, no support ticket and no irreversible mistake.

Questions, answered

Users & Roles, in plain terms

How many user roles does SGEN support? +
Three predefined roles: Administrator (level 1), Editor (level 2), and Customer (level 0). Administrators manage every user and reach every module; Editors get a focused admin with no Users node and author content only; Customers stay on the storefront account portal and never see the admin panel.
Is user management available on all plans? +
Yes. Users & Roles is part of SGEN core, included in every plan from $39/mo, including the free $0 Sandbox. The role model, the audit table, and the recoverable Trash are all native, ready the moment you sign in.
Can I add a teammate without giving them full admin access? +
Yes. Assign the Editor role from the Add User form, where you set the username, name, email, phone, bio, and role. An Editor can author content but never sees the Users table or site settings. Reserve Administrator for the people who run the site.
Can I recover a user I removed by accident? +
Yes. Removing a user soft-deletes it to the Trash tab, where it keeps its data and shows a red Trash tag. Use Restore from the Action For Selected dropdown to bring it back with its role intact, or Delete Permanently when you are sure.
Can I see when an account last logged in? +
Yes. The Users table tracks Last Login and Last IP per account, alongside Created At. Filter to Administrator and run an access review to confirm who holds elevated access and spot any dormant admin.
Does SGEN do custom roles, SSO, or two-factor login? +
No. SGEN ships three fixed roles rather than custom roles or a per-capability permission matrix, and it does not include SSO, SAML, two-factor authentication, or email-invite flows. If your team needs exactly those three levels with native last-login tracking and a recoverable Trash, it is built in; if you need granular custom capabilities, that is not part of the module today.
