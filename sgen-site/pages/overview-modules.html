SG-Modules · 7 categories

22 native modules. Every plugin you pay for, built in.

Build & Design, Content & Publishing, Growth & Conversion, SEO & Performance, Analytics & Attribution, Security & Compliance, and Operations. Seven categories that map onto the WordPress plugin market, because they were built to replace it.

A WordPress site does not start as a plugin stack. It becomes one: a form plugin here, a popup plugin there, a redirect manager, a security scanner, a caching add-on, an SEO suite, each a separate invoice and a separate thing that can break the morning you are not watching. The list below is not a feature tour. It is your current plugin bill, itemized, then deleted.

The whole point

22 modules. Zero plugin licenses. One vendor to call when something breaks.

The WordPress plugin market exists because WordPress shipped without these capabilities. Every plugin you license is a workaround for a gap the platform never closed, maintained by a developer you have never met, on a release schedule you do not control. SGEN was built without those gaps. The 22 modules above are not add-ons layered onto a CMS. They are the platform: they ship with your plan, they update when SGEN updates, and they are tested together.

When something breaks, you make one call, not a triage tour across a dozen vendors arguing over whose update caused it. And the share of an 11,334-vulnerability surface that lands in the plugin layer (91% in 2025, 96% in 2024) is a surface SGEN users simply do not carry, not because plugins are blocked, but because the capabilities they provide are native.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about SGEN modules

How many modules does SGEN include? +
22 native modules across 7 categories: Build & Design (4), Content & Publishing (3), Growth & Conversion (4), SEO & Performance (4), Analytics & Attribution (3), Security & Compliance (2), and Operations (2).
Are all 22 modules included in every plan? +
Yes. All 22 native modules are included in every paid plan, with no feature gating. There are no à-la-carte modules and nothing priced separately; the whole platform ships in the box. The free Sandbox includes all 22 modules too, on unlimited staging-URL sites. Paid plans differ only by how many live sites you can publish: Indie 1, Studio 3, Scale 10, Enterprise unlimited.
Is this just WordPress with the plugins pre-installed? +
No, and the distinction is the whole point. Pre-installed WordPress plugins carry the same update, compatibility, and security surface as any other plugin; they live in wp-content/plugins/ and they show up in the vulnerability data Patchstack tracks. SGEN's 22 modules are native, first-party platform code. They are not plugin files, and they do not appear in that vulnerability surface.
Can I add third-party plugins to SGEN? +
SGEN is not a WordPress site and does not have a plugin ecosystem. In its place you get 22 native modules covering the same capability categories as the WordPress plugin market. When you need something beyond a module, the Custom Codes module injects head and body scripts, and the Integrations module connects to Zapier for workflow automation.
What if SGEN does not have a module for what I need? +
Start by mapping your current plugin list against the 22 modules above; most common plugin categories already correspond to a native module. For genuine gaps, Custom Codes handles script injection and Integrations connects to Zapier, Airtable, Slack, and Google services. For specific requirements beyond that, contact sales to discuss the roadmap.
