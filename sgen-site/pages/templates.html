Templates · library
The whole lifecycle, native

Browse. Apply. Customize. Save your own. Move it anywhere.

The Templates module covers the full lifecycle of a design starting point, from the curated library, to applying a template, to authoring your own, to exporting it for another site. You start every new page from a finished one, not a blank canvas.

Template library

A browsable library of page and block templates. Browse opens a curated external library with Blocks and Pages tabs and a category sidebar, import an item with one click and it joins your library.

Apply, then edit

Apply a template and the SG Builder opens already populated with real sections, type, and layout. Change the words and images that are already laid out instead of building structure and hierarchy from zero each time.

Author your own

Add New builds a fresh template; the edit screen opens any template for customization in the builder. Save your standard hero, your standard pricing layout, your design system becomes a native, reusable asset you own.

Manage the library

Full control of a single template: rename it, duplicate it to branch a variant, trash it, or restore it from trash. Keep the library clean as your design system grows.

Import / export

Export a single template or all published templates in bulk, then import that file on another SGEN site, where it becomes a normal template in that library. Build a starting kit once and reuse it across every site you run.

Section templates

Templates work for whole pages and reusable blocks alike, a footer, a hero, a CTA band you author once and drop into many pages with a [template id="N"] shortcode. Update it in one place and it updates everywhere.

The stack it kills

One native library. The whole template-plugin stack, gone.

On WordPress, starting from a design means bolting on another paid layer, and most teams run more than one in parallel, each with its own license, its own update queue, and its own vulnerability surface. A Divi library leaves with Divi. An Elementor kit is a separate add-on on a separate cadence. An Envato subscription is a recurring bill independent of your hosting and plugins.

Questions

Common questions about Templates

Can I save my own page design as a template? +
Yes. Build or edit your template in the Templates admin, Add New builds a fresh template, and the edit screen opens any template for customization in the SG Builder. Once saved, it appears in your library for reuse on future pages.
Can I share templates across multiple sites? +
Yes, through Import / Export. Export a template from one SGEN site (a single template from its row, or all published templates in bulk) to a file, then import that file on another SGEN site, where it becomes a normal template in that site's library. This is a manual export-then-import, not automatic sync between sites.
What's the difference between a page template and a section template? +
Both live in the same library. A page template is a full-page starting point you apply to a new page. A section template (a "block") is a reusable piece, a footer, a hero, a CTA band, that you author once and drop into many pages by referencing it with a [template id="N"] shortcode. The module is literally titled "templates for pages and blocks."
Do templates only work with the SG Builder? +
Templates are design starting points for the SG Builder, they're made of SG Builder components, and applying one opens a pre-built canvas in the builder for customization. That's the point: a template isn't a static file, it's a live, editable page.
Where do the browse-library templates come from? +
The Browse view loads a curated external library with Blocks and Pages tabs and a category sidebar. Import an item with one click and it becomes a normal template in your library, ready to apply.
Which plan includes Templates? +
Every plan. Templates is one of the 22 native modules, and every plan is the whole platform, plans differ only by how many live sites you run. Paid plans start at $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly). See Compare Plans for current plan-tier details.
