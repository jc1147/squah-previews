Why a native store

WooCommerce is a plugin. So is everything you bolt onto it.

A WooCommerce store is rarely just WooCommerce. To take subscriptions you add an extension. To handle product options you add another. To process payments you add another. Each one is a separate update event, a separate failure surface, and a separate thing to renew, and every one of them lands in the plugin layer the Patchstack report names as the source of nine out of ten WordPress vulnerabilities.

91% of WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins (Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026). That is the real cost of the status quo: not one tool, but a stack of them held in sync by hope, where any single update can take checkout down on a Tuesday afternoon. SGEN ships the store as one native module, products, orders, coupons, checkout, payment config, store layout, and a public storefront, under a single controller, with zero plugins to license or maintain.

Questions

Common questions about Ecommerce

Can I move my existing WooCommerce store to SGEN? +
Moving to SGEN is a rebuild, not an import. There is no product-import tool, and we will not pretend otherwise. You have two real paths: a white-glove rebuild, where we rebuild your store for a quoted price, or a DIY rebuild, where you rebuild on SGEN using the builder and templates. The migration page explains both.
Does SGEN support subscriptions or recurring payments? +
SGEN takes one-time purchases through Stripe and PayPal natively. For subscription or recurring billing, contact sales to discuss your requirements. We would rather scope it with you than overstate it here.
What payment gateways are supported? +
Stripe and PayPal connect natively through the Integrations module, configured in the same store-config area as shipping and tax.
Is ecommerce included, or do I pay extra for it? +
Ecommerce is a native module included in every paid plan. There is nothing extra to buy and no Ecommerce add-on. The free Sandbox includes it too, on staging-URL sites. Every plan is the whole platform; you choose a plan by how many live sites you run: Indie 1, Studio 3, Scale 10, Enterprise unlimited.
