What keeps healthcare operators up at night

Three pressures every healthcare site is under

Accessibility exposure. Plugin-driven security holes. Consent records you can actually produce. None of these is a feature you bolt on after a problem, each is a posture you want in place before one.

Built for the ADA lawsuit surge

Accessibility complaints against websites climbed sharply through 2025, and healthcare practices are a frequent target. A defensible posture starts with the platform: server-rendered pages, audit-ready logging, and compliance-grade infrastructure as the default rather than a setting you hope someone enabled.

What it means: less standing accessibility-litigation exposure, with the defensible posture in the platform before a complaint arrives. Posture, not certification, confirm specifics with compliance counsel.

The number one failure source, removed

On WordPress, the attack surface is the plugin stack: a form plugin, a security plugin, a consent plugin, each on its own update cycle, each a separate way in. For a practice handling sensitive intake, every unpatched plugin is a liability nobody scheduled.

SGEN ships features natively and patches at the platform level. A WAF is included in the Foundation Pack on every plan. There is no security plugin to maintain, and no plugin marketplace to be the way in.

When counsel asks, you have the record

"Do we have a record that this visitor consented?" is a question every regulated site eventually gets. SGEN's Consent & Tracking module captures a per-session record for every banner decision (accept, decline, or no-decision) with the timestamp the banner appeared, the decision time, the landing page, an anonymized IP, and a full chronological timeline.

What it means: you can answer the regulator's question about one visitor on one day. Search by session key, open the record, and print it to PDF, a record you pull on demand.

What SGEN ships natively

The building blocks, built in.

A compliant infrastructure is assembled from a handful of dependable parts. On SGEN, four of them ship natively, not as plugins you license and patch, but as platform capabilities that are simply on.

Web Application Firewall. A platform-level WAF, included in the Foundation Pack and free on every plan, including the $0 Sandbox. Filters malicious traffic at the platform, with no firewall plugin to configure or renew.
Security patching. Platform-managed and centralized. There is no plugin patch cycle for your team to track, and no unpatched plugin to become the way in.
GDPR consent log. A per-session record via the Consent & Tracking module. Every banner decision is captured with timestamps, landing page, anonymized IP, and a full interaction timeline. Searchable, printable, produced on demand.
Audit-ready logging. Platform-level access and action logging across the admin. Combined with the per-session consent records, it gives you the evidence trail regulators and counsel ask for.

What is actually captured in the consent log? +
For every visitor who sees your consent banner, the Consent & Tracking module records a session: the moment the banner appeared, the decision (accept, decline, or no-decision), the decision timestamp, the landing page, the referrer, an anonymized visitor IP (the last segment is zeroed, full IPs are never stored), engagement metrics, and a full chronological timeline of the visitor's interactions. The record is read-only by design: consent records are evidence, not configuration. You search by session key, page, or campaign, open the record, and print it to PDF when counsel asks. It is a record you can produce on demand.
What plan do I need for the GDPR consent log and WAF? +
Any of them. The WAF (via the Foundation Pack) and the consent log are included in every plan, including the free Sandbox. There is no premium tier you have to buy to get a defensible posture; every plan is the whole platform, so pick the plan that fits how many live sites you run, from the free Sandbox up to Enterprise ($497/mo annual; $577 monthly) for unlimited sites.
Can I migrate my existing healthcare site to SGEN? +
On SGEN, migration is a rebuild, not an import: there is no WordPress-content importer. You have two paths: a white-glove rebuild (we rebuild your site on SGEN for you) or a DIY rebuild (you reconstruct it with the SG Builder and templates). For a regulated site this is often the cleaner path, you start on a hardened platform rather than carrying a plugin stack across. Contact us for white-glove pricing.
Is the platform SOC 2 or ISO certified? +
SOC 2 and ISO are in progress. We report status, not certifications we don't hold, and there are no certification badges on this site. See the Trust Center for the current state of SGEN's security and compliance program.
