Analytics & Attribution · SG-Modules

Multi-touch attribution. Call tracking. Native.

WordPress needs CallRail for call tracking and a separate plugin for multi-touch attribution. WP Engine ships nothing here. SGEN builds both into the platform, with no plugin to license and no third-party subscription to renew.

Every dollar you put into a campaign ends with one question: which channel actually produced that lead? On WordPress, answering it is an assembly project: hosting, plus a page builder, plus a CallRail subscription stacked on top, just to see which source drove the phone that rang. On WP Engine there is no answer at all, because native attribution is simply absent. SGEN answers it inside the plan you already pay for. The Attribution module logs every visitor session against its source, organic, paid, referral, direct, and records taps on your phone and SMS links as session events, bucketed by the same source taxonomy.

The WordPress attribution gap

Three invoices. Two integrations. One blind spot.

Attribution on a WordPress stack is not a feature you switch on, it is a system you bolt together. Hosting carries the site, a page builder carries the layout, and a separate service like CallRail carries the one thing your CFO actually asks about: which channel drove the call. That is a third billing relationship, a second integration to maintain, and one more handshake that breaks the next time a plugin or a core update lands.

WP Engine customers have it plainer, and worse: there is no native attribution at any WP Engine tier. The platform is built around managed WordPress hosting, not marketing intelligence, so the moment you need source-of-lead reporting you are back to a third-party subscription, exactly like everyone else on WordPress.

SGEN removes the assembly step. Source attribution and phone-tap logging are part of the platform: one bill, one place to look, one fewer thing that can break on update. Native attribution, no plugin, no CallRail tax.

What Attribution includes

Know which source drove the lead, and the phone tap.

Attribution records the few things a marketing operator genuinely needs to defend a budget, and surfaces them without a second login.

Session tracking

Every visit logged with its source channel, referral domain, landing page, pageview count, and session timing, readable in one list. The traffic behind your numbers stops being a black box.

Source attribution

Six clean buckets, Google Ads, Google Organic, Direct, Referral, Social, Other, derived automatically from UTM and referrer. Filter the whole list to one source to validate a campaign in seconds.

Phone-tap logging

Taps on your tel: and SMS links recorded as session events and attributed to the same six sources. This is tap-level logging, not call recording, caller ID, IVR, or dynamic numbers, and we do not claim to be one.

One session, the whole path

Open a session, see every step that led to the tap.

Each session opens to its own timeline: where it landed, the source it arrived from, the pages it touched, and the moment a visitor tapped your phone or SMS link, logged as an event in the same record. The call interaction shows up in the same place as the web session that produced it. The session list, the settings panel, and this single-session view all ship today.

Compliance layer

Attribution data, collected with a consent log behind it.

Attribution in SGEN runs alongside the Consent & Tracking module: GDPR cookie consent with a per-session consent log for audit. The point is auditability. The consent log records the visitor's decision, the page it was made on, and the timestamp, so when a client or a regulator asks whether you hold a consent record for that session, the answer lives in the log, not in a spreadsheet you rebuild under pressure. Because both records sit in the same platform, the consent decision and the session it governs share one data layer, rather than a separate plugin you hope stayed in sync.

Questions

Questions about Attribution

Does Attribution replace Google Analytics? +
No, and it is not meant to. Google Analytics is a full analytics suite, with audience segments, conversion goals, and cross-property reporting. SGEN Attribution is a first-party session and phone-tap layer native to your site: it tells you the source behind each session and logs taps on your phone links in the same timeline. Run both: connect Google Analytics through the Integrations module, and keep SGEN Attribution for first-party source and tap data you own. SGEN also ships a separate first-party Analytics dashboard for page-view events and top paths.
What call-tracking provider does this replace? +
If the only reason you pay CallRail is to see which source drove your calls, phone-tap logging covers that natively: taps on your tel: and SMS links are recorded and attributed by source, with no separate subscription. It does not record calls, insert dynamic numbers, route calls, or provide caller ID, so if you depend on those specific capabilities, treat SGEN as covering the source-attribution job, not full feature parity with a call-center platform. We would rather be precise than oversell.
Does tracking respect visitor consent? +
Yes. Attribution is paired with the Consent & Tracking module, which logs each visitor's consent decision per session for audit and can gate tracking scripts until consent is given.
