The restaurant reality, and the native fix

The three plugins running your restaurant site are the three things that fail.

A menu plugin, an events plugin, a reservation-form plugin. Three licenses, three logins, three update cycles, and the part of your site that breaks is almost always one of them.

The plugin stack itself

A menu plugin, an events plugin, and a reservation-form plugin mean three things to license and maintain. When an author abandons one, that part of your site goes dark.

The SGEN fix

Menus as Custom Objects, Events native, reservations through native Forms. One platform, no third-party plugins to license or keep current.

Update roulette, live

You push a plugin update before service, a conflict drops your reservation form, and you do not find out until the calls start. WordPress updates are bets you place on your busiest nights.

The SGEN fix

Native features replace the plugins that are the number-one failure source, so there is no plugin-conflict roulette to lose during dinner rush.

A slow site on a phone

Diners check your menu and hours from a phone, often on cell data. A plugin-heavy WordPress site loads slowly, and a slow load is a lost table.

The SGEN fix

Server-side rendering delivers finished content instantly, to users and to Google. The page is ready before the browser paints it, no caching plugin required.

91% of WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins (Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026). The plugin layer is the failure layer. SGEN replaces it with native modules that ship and update with the platform.

Questions

What restaurant owners ask before switching.

Can I show my actual menu, not just a PDF? +
Yes. Your menu is a Custom Object, a native content type for dishes, laid out with the SG Builder. Update a price once and it changes everywhere that dish appears. No PDF, no menu plugin.
Do the reservation forms actually work, and where do the bookings go? +
Forms are native, with bot protection built in. Every reservation request lands in your dashboard inbox, exports to CSV, and shows up in lead reports by source and over time.
I have two locations. Can the site handle that? +
Yes. Each location carries its own address, phone, and per-day hours, and a public location selector lets diners choose their spot. Manage them all from one site.
Can I move my current WordPress restaurant site over? +
Moving to SGEN is a rebuild, not an import, so you do not drag broken menu and events plugins along with you. Two paths: a white-glove rebuild, where we rebuild it for you, or a DIY rebuild on the SG Builder with templates. Contact us for rebuild pricing.
Will the site be fast enough for people checking the menu on their phones? +
That is the point of server-side rendering: the page arrives finished, so it loads fast on cell data, with no caching plugin to configure. Speed counts for Google's ranking too, not just your diners.
