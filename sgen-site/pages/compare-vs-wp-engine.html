The honest cost comparison

Start at the same baseline. Then look at the next two layers up.

WP Engine includes basic SSL, a CDN, and L3/L4 DDoS protection on every plan. That is a real, production-grade baseline, and SGEN includes the same essentials free, on every plan, even the $0 Sandbox. So far, no contest either way. The contrast is the two layers above it.

Layer one: the firewall

The WAF

WP Engine's web application firewall ships inside Global Edge Security, which is a paid add-on. A third-party estimate puts it at roughly $300 to $450 a year. On SGEN, the web application firewall and security patching are in the platform, free on every plan, including the $0 Sandbox. There is no separate firewall line item to buy.

What it means: their security tax starts where yours is free. You get the WAF without buying back the layer as a separate add-on on top of your plan.

Layer two: attribution

Native attribution

WP Engine has no native attribution. WordPress and WP Engine operators reach for a tool like CallRail, a separate subscription on top of the hosting bill. SGEN ships attribution natively: multi-touch attribution and call tracking, with no third-party attribution tax.

What it means: the attribution that WP Engine operators pay a third party for is already in the platform. One stack, no add-on, so you see which campaign produced a lead without a separate line item.

Questions

Common questions about SGEN vs WP Engine

Does SGEN replace WordPress or WP Engine? +
Both. WP Engine is managed hosting for WordPress, so it sits on top of WordPress core and a plugin stack. SGEN replaces the CMS (WordPress) and the managed-hosting layer (WP Engine) at once. You get one native platform instead of a CMS, a hosting vendor, a firewall add-on, and a pile of plugins.
Is WP Engine secure enough? +
Yes. WP Engine is a production-grade managed-WordPress host. They include basic SSL, a CDN, and L3/L4 DDoS protection on every plan, and they harden the platform around WordPress. We are not going to tell you otherwise. The honest difference is two layers up: their web application firewall ships in Global Edge Security, which is a paid add-on (a third-party estimate puts it at roughly $300 to $450 a year), and they have no native attribution, so you typically add a tool like CallRail separately. On SGEN, the WAF and security patching are free on every plan, and attribution is a native module. And no matter how well the platform is hardened, it is still WordPress underneath: 91% of 2025 WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins (Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026 report). SGEN has no plugin substrate to defend.
What will happen to my content? +
Moving to SGEN is a rebuild, not an import, so your content does not transfer automatically. You rebuild your pages on SGEN, either yourself (DIY rebuild with SG Builder and the template library) or with our team (white-glove rebuild). The upside: you leave the WordPress plugin substrate behind and start on a clean native architecture. To protect your search rankings through the move, the native Redirects module lets you map every old URL to its new home so you keep your link equity. See the migration guide for the full rebuild walk-through.
What does the free baseline include, and is it really free? +
Hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, security patching, and a multi-site dashboard, free on every plan, including the $0 Sandbox. It is not a tier or an add-on; it is the baseline that ships with every account. On a WordPress and WP Engine stack, the firewall (Global Edge Security) is a paid add-on and the multi-site setup depends on your plan.
What plugins and subscriptions does SGEN replace? +
In a typical WordPress and WP Engine stack, SGEN replaces: the firewall add-on (native WAF plus security patching), security plugins like Wordfence and Sucuri, attribution tools like CallRail (native Attribution module), form plugins like WPForms or Gravity Forms (Forms module), popup plugins like OptinMonster (Popups module), and SEO plugins like Yoast (On-Page Optimization module). All native: 22 modules across 7 categories, no plugin licenses to stack.
