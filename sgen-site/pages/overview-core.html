The WordPress problem

A content team on WordPress is a stack of plugins. Each one is an update vector.

Set up a content-team site on WordPress and the bill comes due in plugins: a page-builder plugin to edit a page, an image-optimization plugin for WebP, a permissions plugin for roles, a mega-menu plugin for navigation, a theme-builder plugin for global design. Every one updates on its own schedule, and every update is a chance for the site to break.

91% of WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins (Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026; 11,334 vulnerabilities in full-2025 data, 91% plugins, 9% themes, 6 in core; the pattern holds year over year, 96% in 2024 and 91% in 2025). SG-Core replaces that stack with native capabilities on one platform. You maintain your whole content stack as one product, instead of reconciling five plugins that each update, and each break, on their own timeline.

Questions, answered

SG-Core, in plain terms

Does SG-Core replace Gutenberg and a page-builder plugin? +
Yes. SG-Core ships a native page and blog engine, and SG Builder is a native visual editor. There is no WordPress core, no Gutenberg, and no page-builder plugin underneath. You build pages and publish posts inside the platform itself.
Is SG-Core available on the free Sandbox? +
Yes. SG-Core is part of every plan, including the free Sandbox, and the Foundation Pack is included on the Sandbox too. The whole platform ships on every plan; the only thing a Sandbox site cannot do is go public-live on a custom domain, since it runs on a staging URL. Upgrade to a paid plan for that.
How many native modules come with the platform? +
22 native modules across 7 categories, on top of the six SG-Core content areas and the SG-Dashboard operating layer. None of them is a third-party plugin.
Do I still need plugins for media optimization, roles, or menus? +
No. Media optimization (WebP and AVIF), role-based access control, and the visual menu builder are all native SG-Core capabilities, managed in the same dashboard, with nothing to install and nothing to keep current.
Is the Theme Editor a live capability today? +
Yes. The Theme Editor capability is live in the admin: typography, global colors, layout, and your header and footer are all editable site-wide. It is part of SG-Core, managed in the same dashboard as your pages and posts.
