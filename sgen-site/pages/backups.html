What the backup system gives you

Every saved backup is a full-site restore point.

A backup captures the whole site into one .sgen archive: pages, posts, products, settings, and media. The day something breaks, that snapshot is the difference between a short, known recovery and a week of rebuilding from memory.

Create on demand

One click captures the full site into a .sgen archive. Save a snapshot before any risky change, a theme swap, a bulk edit, an integration that writes data, and the worst case becomes a one-click revert.

Restore to any point

Pick a snapshot, restore, and your live site is back to exactly that state. Restore replaces the current site, so it is a deliberate action, save a fresh backup right before restoring an older one.

Rename for future-you

Label a snapshot so it is findable later, before-theme-swap.sgen beats a timestamp. The system normalizes the name and keeps the .sgen extension intact.

Download off-site

Pull a copy to your own computer or cloud storage for a real disaster-recovery layer. The archive otherwise lives on the same disk as the site, so a downloaded copy is your true off-site safety net.

Retention list

Each snapshot stays in your retention list until you remove it. You decide the timing, snapshot right before the risky move, and keep the restore points that matter.

Delete, single or bulk

Clear archives you no longer need to reclaim space, one at a time or in bulk. Full row control over every restore point, all from one panel.

No backup plugin to maintain

The whole backup stack, part of the platform.

Backups are part of the platform, not a plugin you install and keep current plus a third-party storage account you wire up and pay for. You get a working backup system the moment you have a site, with nothing to configure beyond clicking Create.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about Backups

Does this replace UpdraftPlus or VaultPress? +
Yes, for SGEN sites, backup and restore are native. There is no backup plugin to install, license, or keep updated, and no third-party storage account to configure. It ships with the platform and updates with the platform.
Is restore reversible? +
Restore replaces your current site with the snapshot you chose, so it is deliberate by design. The standard practice is to save a fresh backup right before restoring an older one, so you always have a way forward and a way back.
Can I migrate my WordPress site with this? +
No, this is SGEN backup and restore only. It saves and restores snapshots of your SGEN content. Moving from WordPress to SGEN is a rebuild, white-glove or DIY, not an import. See the rebuild paths on the migrate page.
Where are my backups stored, and how do I get an off-site copy? +
Each archive lives with the site by default, so for true disaster recovery you download a copy to your own computer or cloud storage. A downloaded .sgen archive is your real off-site safety layer, and a meaningful filename makes the right one easy to find later.
Which plan includes backups? +
Backups are one of the 22 native modules, included in every plan, from $39/mo (billed annually; $47 monthly). The free Sandbox runs backups too, on unlimited staging sites, so you can try a snapshot and restore before you pay anything.
