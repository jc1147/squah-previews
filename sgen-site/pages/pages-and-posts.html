The whole authoring workflow, native

Open a page, write, publish. Nothing underneath to maintain.

WordPress turned its own CMS into a plugin problem: a page builder to edit a page, a scheduling plugin to time a post, an SEO plugin just to set a slug. SGEN ships the entire page and blog engine in SG-Core, which is part of every plan.

One structured tree

Every page in one list, grouped into All, Published, Draft, and Trash, searchable by title and sortable by date or name. A 200-page site stays manageable by one person, find any page in seconds and act on it inline.

Revision history

Every content save snapshots the previous version. Preview any of the last ten saved versions in a separate tab, then restore the right one in one click. Version safety natively, with no revisions plugin and no inflated promise to distrust.

Schedule and duplicate

Manage status from the list or the editor: draft, publish, or schedule for later. Duplicate a page and the copy starts as a draft, so you edit it without touching the original. Trash and restore are recoverable until you permanently delete.

Per-page SEO and slug

Each page owns its meta title, meta description, social image, and slug, all edited inside the page editor. You set on-page SEO and the URL for any page without an SEO plugin.

A real publishing engine

Posts run on a full post type with categories, tags, scheduling, and per-post layout. Run an editorial calendar, write ahead and schedule posts to go live later, without a scheduling plugin or an editorial-workflow plugin.

Set as Homepage

Swap which page serves at the site root in a click. The old homepage stays as a normal page at its slug, so changing your front door never loses the page behind it.

Posts / Blog

A publishing engine, posts, categories, scheduling, without a plugin.

The blog list works like the Pages list, filter by status, search, paginate, and act on rows inline. Behind it is a full post type: assign a Single Template per post and an Archive Template for the listing, choose how post URLs are built, organize by category and tag, and turn on a promo banner above the archive for a campaign. You publish like a media operation, not like someone fighting a CMS.

On WordPress this is assembled from a page-builder plugin to edit, a scheduling or editorial plugin to time posts, and an SEO plugin to control slugs and meta. Each is a separate license and a separate update.

Questions, answered

Pages and Posts, in plain terms

Does SGEN replace Gutenberg? +
Yes. SGEN ships a native page and blog engine, and pages are edited in SG Builder, so there is no WordPress core and no Gutenberg underneath. You write and publish inside the platform itself, with no page-builder plugin to maintain.
Is revision history available on all plans? +
Pages and Posts, including revision history, are part of SG-Core, which is included in every plan from $39/mo, and on the free Sandbox. Revision history keeps the most recent ten saved versions per page and restores the body content of any of them.
Can I schedule posts to publish later? +
Yes. Posts support draft, publish, and scheduled status natively, with categories, tags, and permalink control. No scheduling plugin required.
What happens to old URLs if I change a slug or permalink structure? +
Changing a slug or the site permalink structure changes the affected URLs site-wide, and old URLs will 404 unless you add a redirect, stated up front so you plan it instead of discovering it. SGEN includes a native Redirects manager for exactly this; it is not automatic, so add the redirect when you make the change.
Do I need separate plugins for pages and for the blog? +
No. Pages and Posts are one native authoring layer in SG-Core. The same dashboard manages both, with no separate licenses and nothing to keep current.
