Finally a WordPress alternative.

The standard stack, WordPress core, Elementor for design, WP Engine for hosting, works until it does not. A plugin breaks on update. A hole opens in a tool you are paying for. Your Lighthouse score tanks while the hosting bill climbs.

SGEN is built to eliminate those failure modes at the root: 22 modules shipped natively, server-side rendering by default, and the infrastructure stack included free on every plan. Below are the two arguments for the switch, each on its own proof page.

The wedge

A WordPress site is rarely one product. It is a dozen of them.

A working WordPress site is usually a page builder, plus an SEO plugin, a forms plugin, caching, backups, popups, a redirect manager, and more. Each is a separate license to renew, a separate update that can land badly, and a separate codebase you did not write running on the site your business depends on. SGEN is built the other way around: the features you would reach for a plugin to add ship as native modules, so there is no plugin layer to license, patch, or keep compatible.

One bill, not a stack of subscriptions. Every plugin category above is a separate invoice. With SGEN the whole platform is what you get, and every plan includes all 22 modules.
One update cadence, not a dozen. Modules update with the platform, so there is no separate calendar per plugin and no dependency tree to break on a Tuesday.
Less standing exposure. Removing the plugin layer removes the surface most WordPress vulnerabilities live in, the single largest category Patchstack counts.

Server-first speed and stability

Server-side rendering and Smart-Loading Logic deliver fast, interactive pages without a caching plugin. Your server sends finished HTML, not a bundle the visitor's browser has to execute. Immutable Stability means platform updates do not touch your content or your design.

Patchstack, State of WordPress Security 2026

91% of WordPress vulnerabilities were in plugins.

Not in core, not in your theme. Across the full year of 2025, Patchstack catalogued 11,334 WordPress vulnerabilities: 91% in plugins, 9% in themes, 6 in core. On a stack of 10 to 30 plugins that exposure is not theoretical, it is structural. SGEN removes the plugin layer entirely, so there is no plugin exposure because there are no plugins to expose.

The infrastructure others charge for is the baseline here.

Foundation Pack ships on every plan, even the $0 Sandbox: hosting, SSL, CDN, WAF, security patching, and the multi-site dashboard. You launch on a CDN, behind a WAF, on managed SSL before you write a line of copy. WP Engine includes SSL and CDN on all tiers, that is honest and worth knowing.
