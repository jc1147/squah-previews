01The build-it-yourself trap

Sourcing consistent distillate yourself is a second supply chain.

Standing up a reliable distillate supply on your own means chasing labs, vetting potency and purity batch by batch, and absorbing the same headcount math that traps brands in a manual roll room. A semi-manual operation runs on a 6-10 person crew; that 10-person payroll, the equipment, and the compliance overhead all land before a single liter is consistent. And inconsistent distillate ripples downstream, every cart, edible and infused SKU it feeds inherits the variance. BatchWorks turns the hunt into one coordinated relationship you plug into, instead of a vendor search you re-run every quarter.

  • Batch-by-batch potency and purity vetting before a single liter is consistent.
  • A 10-person payroll, the equipment, and a second compliance surface to own.
  • Inconsistent distillate ripples into every cart, edible and infused SKU it feeds.

07Straight answers

Distillate, answered plainly.

Q

Is distillate produced in-house?

No. Distillate is partner-facilitated: we coordinate vetted partners to your spec and hold them to a COA per lot. The in-house line is pre-roll. We name it plainly rather than imply a line we do not operate.

Q

What grades, and what lead times?

THC, CBD, minor-cannabinoid and terpene-reintroduced grades, quoted per program against the partner schedule; a pilot can move on a short 24-hour scope turnaround once the specs are set.

Q

Is there a minimum order?

No minimums. There is no 1,000-unit floor: run a pilot batch or a full launch on the same path.

08How pricing works

A variable per-unit cost, quoted to your program.

Distillate is priced the way the whole shop is priced: a variable per-unit cost quoted to your grade mix, volume and finish. It is quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate depends on your program. The point is the shape of the cost, not a sticker price.

What the quote model gives you.

A separate distillate vendor relationship becomes a variable per-unit cost you spend only when you produce. No minimums and no 1,000-unit floor, so a pilot and a full launch are priced on the same path, scaled to your volume. Tell us your SKUs and your volume and the quote follows your grade mix.
