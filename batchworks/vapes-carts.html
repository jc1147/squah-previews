01The build-it-yourself trap

Standing up a vape line in-house is a capital project, not a SKU.

Adding carts the hard way starts with sourcing the hardware, cartridges, batteries and ceramic coils, then building the fill, cap and crimp capacity to run it. That is the same headcount math that traps brands in a manual roll room: a semi-manual operation runs on a 6-10 person crew, and a 10-person payroll, the equipment, and a second compliance surface all land before a single cartridge ships.

07Straight answers

Carts, answered plainly.

Q

Is filling operated in-house?

No. Carts is partner-facilitated: we hold vetted filling partners to your spec and a COA per lot. The in-house line is pre-roll. We name it plainly rather than imply a line we do not operate.

Q

What hardware can I run?

510-thread carts, all-in-one disposables, and pod formats, in standard capacities, specced to your brand and matched to your device where you run one.

Q

Minimums and lead times?

No minimums, and no 1,000-unit floor; a pilot can move on a short 24-hour scope turnaround once specs are set. No queue-jumping promises we cannot keep.

08How pricing works

A variable per-unit cost, quoted to your program.

Carts is priced the way the whole shop is priced: a simple variable per-unit cost quoted to your format mix, volume and finish. It is quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate depends on your program. The point is the shape of the cost, not a sticker price.

What the quote model gives you.

A fixed fill payroll becomes a variable per-unit cost you spend only when you produce. No minimums and no 1,000-unit floor, so a pilot and a full launch are priced on the same path, scaled to your volume. Tell us your SKUs and your volume and the quote follows your format mix.
