Within FY26 the market is essentially flat in a ~$40-46M/month band, with no in-year decline. The softening story is year over year, not month to month (see below). April captures the 4/20 demand spike; summer months (May-Jun, tourism-driven) are not yet posted.

Clark dominates the state market

Latest reported month (Apr 2026), Nevada taxable sales by county.
Clark 73.8%

Clark County ($45.7M) is roughly 5x Washoe/Reno ($8.7M) and dwarfs all other counties combined ($7.6M). Practical implication: the statewide Nevada market is effectively the Clark market, so any statewide data source (e.g. Headset) closely reflects Clark, and Clark is the correct benchmark for normalizing any Las Vegas client.

Clark's FY26 run-rate ($42.2M/mo) annualizes to ~$506M, which would put Clark roughly 11% below FY25 and continues the multi-year decline. The contraction is consistent with a maturing, price-compressing market, not a one-off.

Caveat: the FY26 annualized figures assume the two unposted months (May-Jun) run at the YTD average. Summer in Las Vegas is tourism-heavy and often above average, so the true FY26 number may land a few points better than -11%. Treat as directional.

Read: every category is down year over year in both months. Concentrates (-23% to -26%) and vapor (-20% to -21%) are the weakest; flower - the largest category at ~41% of the market - is among the most defensive (-16% to -17%); beverages are tiny (~1.5%) but the most resilient. The mix is stable month to month and heavily inhalable (flower + vapor + pre-rolls = ~82% of the market).

Method + caveat: no source publishes Clark-by-category directly. Clark dollars = Headset NV category x Clark's monthly share of NV (~74%). The Headset-basis total (~$37.9M, Apr) sits below the Clark tax taxable-sales figure ($45.7M) because the tax data includes medical and other taxable items while Headset measures cannabis-product retail - so treat the category mix and YoY as the reliable signal and the dollar splits as estimates. Methodology, sources, and caveats

  • Source: Nevada Department of Taxation, "Cannabis Tax Revenue" report (FY26, county breakdown), last updated 6/23/2026; annual figures from the CCB / Taxation joint release (Feb 2026). These are tax-filing actuals, not a forecast panel.
  • "Taxable sales" includes adult-use cannabis, medical cannabis, and other tangible personal property subject to sales/use tax at licensed dispensaries. It is broader than retail cannabis alone, but it is the standard CCB/Taxation market figure and is internally consistent month to month.
  • Data note - adult-use retail back-estimate is unreliable. Dividing the 10% retail excise by 0.10 overshoots total taxable sales (FY26 YTD it implies ~$430.6M adult-use vs $421.9M total taxable, which is impossible). The Department notes monthly excise does not equal 10% of taxable sales due to base and timing/delinquency differences. Use taxable sales for sizing; do not use the excise back-estimate.
  • Lag: the report runs ~6-8 weeks behind; the most recent 1-2 months are typically blank.
