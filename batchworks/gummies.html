01The build-it-yourself trap

Standing up gummy production is a confectionery line, not a SKU.

Making gummies the hard way means building your own dosing, depositing and curing capacity, plus the kitchen, the equipment, and a second compliance surface for every milligram. It is the same headcount math that traps brands in a manual roll room: a semi-manual operation runs on a 6-10 person crew, and a 10-person payroll lands before a single gummy ships. That is fixed cost you carry whether or not the SKU sells. BatchWorks turns it into something you plug into instead of something you staff up.

  • A whole confectionery floor: dosing, depositing and curing capacity to build and run.
  • A 10-person payroll, equipment, and a second compliance surface for every milligram.
  • Fixed cost carried whether or not the SKU sells.

07Straight answers

Gummies, answered plainly.

Q

Is gummy production operated in-house?

No, partner-facilitated. We coordinate vetted confectionery partners to your spec; the in-house line is pre-roll. We name it plainly rather than imply a line we do not operate.

Q

How are dosing and compliance handled?

Every piece is checked for its dose and potency before release, and the verified result is recorded on the COA that ships with the order.

Q

Lead times and minimums?

Quoted per program; a pilot moves on a short 24-hour scope turnaround once specs are set. No minimums, and no 1,000-unit floor: run a pilot batch or a full launch the same way.

08How pricing works

A variable per-unit cost, quoted to your program.

Gummies are priced the way the whole shop is priced: a variable per-unit cost quoted to your format mix, dose and volume. It is quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate depends on your program. The point is the shape of the cost, not a sticker price.

What the quote model gives you.

A fixed confectionery payroll becomes a cost you carry only when you run a batch. No minimums, so a pilot and a full launch are priced on the same path, scaled to your volume. Tell us your SKUs and your volume and the quote follows your format mix.
