01How the model works

One per-unit number. Quoted to your program.

Pricing is the same model that prices the whole shop: a simple per-unit number set to your format mix, SKU depth, volume and infusion. Three principles hold it together.

02Why a per-unit quote pays off

Fixed payroll becomes a cost you spend only when you produce.

Here is the math behind the model. An in-house roll room runs on a 6-10 person crew, a 10-person payroll you carry every week whether the line is running or sitting idle. That is fixed cost against demand you cannot always predict. A per-unit quote converts that fixed roll-room payroll into a cost you spend only when you produce.

And the yield discipline pays part of the freight. The in-house pre-roll flagship holds a measurable yield of about ~95% on average, versus 80-85% on a manual run. That 95% yield gap is recovered flower, saleable units that offset what the service costs instead of grams lost to a sloppy line.
