How it works · the per-strain ecosystem

There is no one machine for every pre-roll.

So we do not run one. BatchWorks is a per-strain ecosystem: each strain and SKU routed to the equipment tuned for it, then finished with a final human check. The result is yield a roll room cannot hold, on the formats your shelf actually needs.

01What one-process-fits-all costs you

A roll room forces every strain through one recipe, and the burn shows it.

The in-house roll room runs on semi-manual cone-filling machines and a 6-10 person crew, all tuned to a single setting. But flower is not uniform: density, moisture, and trichome load shift strain to strain. Push them all through one mill speed and one fill pressure and you get canoeing, clogs, and uneven burn, the defects that come back as returns and lost shelf trust. A bigger crew can work harder, but it cannot make one setting fit every strain.

  • One mill speed and one fill pressure, applied to every strain alike.
  • Canoeing, clogs, and uneven burn returning as defects and refunds.
  • A 10-person crew that hits a structural ceiling, not a staffing one.
6-10 person crew a single-recipe roll room runs
02Watch the recipe change with the strain

One strain in. Its own recipe out.

Here is the per-strain ecosystem working. A dense, resinous indica and a light, fluffy sativa do not share settings, so they do not share a line state. Load the strain, call its recipe, and the equipment reconfigures to it before the run starts, then a human-QC touch closes every run.

04Stage 1 · intake & conditioning

It starts before the grind, at intake and moisture.

1

Logged by strain

Every lot enters under METRC and is logged by strain before anything touches it, so the recipe and the chain of custody both start at the door.

2

Conditioned to target

We read moisture and bring each batch to its target window, because flower too dry shatters into fines and flower too wet clogs the mill and burns hot. Get it right and grind, fill, and burn all fall into spec.

3

Where R&D begins

This is also where idea-to-shelf R&D begins: a new format or recipe is dialed in here, at the same intake bench the production lots run through.

05Stage 2 · grind & mill

The grind that protects what you paid for.

Milling runs on stainless mills sized per strain, not one blade for everything. A coarse, dense strain and a delicate, frosty one draw different sizes and speeds from each strain's recipe, each cut to its own uniform fraction with the trichome heads intact.

Sized per strain

Mill size and speed drawn from the strain's recipe, not one blade forced onto every lot.

Particle uniformity

An even grind that airflows and burns straight, the foundation of a cone that does not canoe.

Trichomes intact

Cut to its uniform fraction without tearing the trichomes that carry potency and aroma.

Checked off the line

An operator samples particle size and trichome condition off the line, so uniformity is confirmed, not assumed.

06Stage 3 · homogenize, fill & finish

Even blend, even fill, finished to a hand-checked spec.

Ground flower is homogenized so every cone draws from the same consistent blend: no hot pockets, no weak ends. The line sets the spec; a person signs off that the unit met it, because a human-QC touch is the last gate before packaging.

  • Homogenized blend, so every cone draws the same fraction end to end.
  • Low-temp infusion on infused SKUs, protecting the terpene and cannabinoid profile.
  • Filled to weight, then closed with a Dutch Crown twist for a clean retail finish.
  • An operator checks fill weight, density, and the finish on pulled units.
Dutch Crown finish, then a hand-checked gate
