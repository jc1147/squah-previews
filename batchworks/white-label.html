01The build-it-yourself trap

Launching a branded SKU should not mean building a factory first.

Putting a new SKU on the shelf under your own brand the hard way means standing up production yourself, the same headcount math that traps a brand in a manual roll room. A semi-manual operation runs on a 6-10 person crew; a 10-person payroll, the equipment, and the licensing overhead all land before a single unit ships. That is fixed cost you carry whether or not the SKU sells, and it pulls your team off the brand work that grows the business.

  • Headcount: a 6-10 person crew before the first unit ships.
  • A 10-person payroll, equipment depreciation, and a second compliance surface to keep clean.

02What you get, what you keep

Your brand stays yours. Spec-controlled. Scaled to your volume.

White-label routes through one full-service partner, so you add a branded SKU without building or staffing a line of your own. You keep the decisions that make it your brand; we run the line that produces it.

Your brand stays yours

You keep every brand, sales, and product decision; we run the line. This is a real manufacturing partner, not supplemental labor, not a co-packer you hand scraps to. Your recipe, your label, your shelf relationships stay in your hands.

Spec-controlled

You set the format, the potency target, and the finish; we hold the build to it with QC on every run. Producing under your label does not mean handing off control of it.

Scalable, no floor

Start with a small pilot or open at full volume on one path, and scale the order up as the SKU sells, without re-staffing a line each time demand moves.

04What the discipline buys you

The same yield discipline, applied to your product.

Recovered grams are recovered margin: the gap between a sloppy run and a controlled one is finished product you can sell.

07Straight answers

White-label, answered plainly.

Q

What do I actually own?

Your brand, end to end: the recipe and spec, the label, the sales relationships. We run the production; you own everything that makes it yours.

Q

Is there a minimum order?

No minimums. There is no 1,000-unit floor to clear, so a pilot batch and a production run share the same path.

Q

What are the lead times?

Quoted per program; a pilot can move on a short 24-hour scope turnaround once your specs are set. No queue-jumping promises we cannot keep.

08How pricing works

A variable per-unit cost, quoted to your program.

White-label is priced the way the whole shop is priced: a simple variable per-unit cost quoted to your format mix, SKU depth, and volume. It is quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate depends on your program.

What the quote model gives you.

Convert a roll-room payroll into a per-unit number you spend only when you produce. No minimums and no 1,000-unit floor, so a pilot and a full launch price the same way, scaled to your volume.
