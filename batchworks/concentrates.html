01The build-it-yourself trap

Standing up extraction in-house is a capital project, not a SKU.

Adding a concentrate line the hard way means sourcing or building extraction capacity yourself, the same headcount math that traps brands in a manual roll room. A semi-manual operation runs on a 6-10 person crew; a 10-person payroll, equipment, and the licensing overhead all land before a single gram ships. That is fixed cost you carry whether or not the SKU sells. BatchWorks turns it into something you plug into instead of something you staff up.

  • Extraction headcount: a 6-10 person crew before the first gram ships.

07Straight answers

Concentrates, answered plainly.

Q

Is extraction operated in-house?

No. Concentrates is partner-facilitated: we coordinate vetted extraction partners to your spec and hold them to a COA per lot. The in-house line is pre-roll. We name it plainly rather than imply a line we do not operate.

Q

What are the lead times?

Quoted per program against the partner schedule; a pilot can move on a short 24-hour scope turnaround once specs are set. No queue-jumping promises we cannot keep.

Q

Is there a minimum order?

No minimums. There is no 1,000-unit floor: run a pilot batch or a full launch on the same path.

08How pricing works

A variable per-unit cost, quoted to your program.

Concentrates is priced the way the whole shop is priced: a simple variable per-unit cost quoted to your format mix, volume and finish. It is quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate depends on your program. The point is the shape of the cost, not a sticker price.

What the quote model gives you.

A fixed extraction payroll becomes a variable per-unit cost you spend only when you produce. No minimums and no 1,000-unit floor, so a pilot and a full launch are priced on the same path, scaled to your volume. Tell us your SKUs and your volume and the quote follows your format mix.
