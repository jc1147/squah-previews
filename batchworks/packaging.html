Packaging & labeling · handled with production

Compliant packaging and labeling. In lockstep with production.

Cannabis packaging and labeling handled as part of the run, not bolted on after it. Labels applied, cases labeled, and units finished in-house at QC; the components themselves sourced and coordinated to your spec. Nothing on the line waits on supply, and every unit leaves compliant.

01The supply-and-compliance scramble

Packaging that arrives out of sync with your biomass is dead capital.

When components are sourced on a separate track from production, they land on a different clock than your flower: early, sitting as cash on a shelf, or late, stalling a finished run that is otherwise ready to ship. The manual fix is always more people, the same headcount math that traps brands in a roll room, where a semi-manual operation leans on a 6-10 person crew to keep up. A 10-person payroll spent chasing tubes, labels, and shifting Nevada compliance is fixed cost that produces nothing. BatchWorks folds packaging into the production run itself, so supply never sets the schedule.

03What we handle

Tubes, jars, mylar, labels. Packed and labeled at QC.

The same end-to-end line that runs pre-roll from idea-to-shelf R&D through finished-product QC also handles the labeling and packing on the way out the door. Components are sourced to spec and coordinated to the run; labels and cases are finished on our own floor.

1

Components staged

Tubes, jars and mylar matched to format and potency, sourced and coordinated to spec. Component supply is partner-facilitated, so nothing on the line waits on a PO.

2

Filled on the run

Units are filled and finished on the same run, not bolted on after it, so the SKU moves through as one job.

3

Labels applied

Compliant face and back labels go on at the QC step, checked to current Nevada rules before a unit ships.

4

Master-case labeling

Case and carton labels go on at the same gate, every master case marked and ready to load.

5

Finished-product QC

A final finished-product QC, then tubing and shipping, so the lot ships compliant.

05What this replaces

Replaces managing packaging supply and compliance yourself.

Routing packaging through BatchWorks replaces standing up your own packaging-and-compliance function: the buyer chasing components, the parts bought ahead of demand and stored against a forecast, the staff tracking Nevada's shifting labeling rules. That is the same overhead that bloats a manual roll room into a 6-10 person crew. Instead of a 10-person line of fixed cost carried before a single unit sells, packaging becomes a coordinated step inside the production run you already pay for.

  • No packaging buyer chasing components and labels week to week.
  • No parts bought ahead of demand and stored against a forecast.
  • No separate compliance desk tracking Nevada's shifting rules.

07Straight answers

Packaging, answered plainly.

Q

Is the packaging compliant for Nevada?

Yes. Compliance is built into the run: cases and labels are checked at QC for Nevada before anything leaves the floor.

Q

What are the lead times?

Quoted per program against the supply schedule; a pilot can move on a short 24-hour scope turnaround once specs and components are set.

Q

Is there a minimum order?

No minimums. There is no 1,000-unit floor: a pilot batch and a full production run move the same way.

Q

What materials do you handle?

Tubes, jars and mylar, plus compliant label application and case labeling at QC.

08How pricing works

A variable per-unit cost, quoted to your program.

Packaging and labeling roll into your program's variable per-unit cost: quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate depends on your SKUs and your volume. The point is the shape of the cost, not a sticker price.

What the quote model covers.

A fixed packaging-and-compliance payroll becomes a per-unit number you pay only as you produce. There are no minimums, so a pilot and a launch are priced the same, scaled to your volume. Tell us your SKUs and your volume and the quote follows your format mix.
