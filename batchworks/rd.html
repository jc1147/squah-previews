Product development · idea to shelf

From idea to shelf. R&D at production scale.

Take a concept all the way to a shelf-ready SKU, without building a lab or standing up a line. This is idea-to-shelf R&D run where it counts: formulate, prototype, test and deploy on the same line that runs your production, so what you prove in development is exactly what ships at volume.

01The prototype-to-production gap

A great prototype is not 10,000 consistent units.

The hard part of a new product is never the first sample. It is everything between a winning bench prototype and ten thousand finished units that come out identical, lot after lot. Most brands cross that gap by hand: a developer dials a recipe one way, the floor rebuilds it another, and the SKU that ships is not the SKU you approved.

02Why develop where you produce

Ideate, formulate, prototype, on the line that runs production.

Development happens on the exact equipment that fills your runs, so the formula you settle in the lab is the formula the floor already knows how to make. No second build, no re-interpretation, no surprise when the volume order lands.

One line, no handoff

You ideate, formulate and prototype on the equipment that fills your runs, so nothing gets re-interpreted between the bench and the floor.

Proven at scale

A formula that holds on one hand-built sample can fall apart at volume. Developing at production scale makes the sample a proof of the run, not a hopeful preview of it.

No capital project

Because the work shares equipment you already run, you trial new formats and SKUs without buying machines or staffing a separate development crew.

What you approve, ships

Sign-off is on the real process: the spec you approve is exactly what reproduces when the volume order turns the line up.

03The full path

Ideate, formulate, prototype, test, deploy at scale.

Idea-to-shelf R&D runs one unbroken path on the real production pipeline: intake and METRC compliance, biomass prep, grind and homogenize, dosing, fill and finish, dual weight checks with rejection QC, then finished-product QC and labeling. Your SKU is graded against real fill and real checks, never a lab approximation, and deploying is the same line turned up.

05What this replaces

Replaces building a lab to develop one SKU.

Doing it the conventional way means building the capability before you prove the product: a lab or a new facility, equipment to depreciate, and a development team on payroll. It is the same trap as the in-house roll room, a semi-manual operation that runs on a 6-10 person crew you carry whether or not the launch lands. Route the work through BatchWorks and you develop on a line that already exists, paying for the work, not the building.

  • No lab build-out and no new facility to stand up first.
  • No 10-person development payroll carried before a SKU is proven.
  • You keep the formula, the brand and the SKU; they stay yours.

07Straight answers

Development, answered plainly.

Q

What do you bring, and what do we?

You bring the concept, the strain and the target. We bring the line and the process that turns it into a repeatable, shelf-ready unit.

You do. The recipe, the brand and the finished SKU stay yours; we are the line you develop them on, nothing more.

08How pricing works

Development is priced the way the whole shop is: a variable per-unit cost set against your formats, volume and finish. It is quote-only pricing, no public number, because the right rate follows the program, not a sticker.
